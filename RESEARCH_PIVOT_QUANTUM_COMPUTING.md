## Next Arc: Quantum Computing Mechanisms

### The Question

How do Synchronism's core analogies inform a new perspective on quantum computing mechanisms, with the goal of **specific implementable technologies/methods**?

### Key Analogies to Explore

#### 1. The CRT Analogy

In Synchronism, the CRT (cathode ray tube) analogy describes how:
- A single electron beam creates the appearance of a full image through rapid scanning
- The "image" doesn't exist all at once - it's constructed through temporal coherence
- The observer's persistence of vision completes the illusion

**Quantum computing question**: Are qubits more like the electron beam (actual) or the phosphor dots (potential)? What if quantum computation is fundamentally about **temporal coherence** rather than spatial superposition?

#### 2. The Pendulum Clock Analogy

Synchronized pendulum clocks on a shared wall will eventually phase-lock through mechanical coupling. This demonstrates:
- Coherence emerging from weak coupling
- No central controller needed
- Stability through resonance, not rigidity

**Quantum computing question**: Current approaches fight decoherence. What if we **use** decoherence as the coupling mechanism? Can computation emerge from managed phase-locking rather than isolated qubits?

### Research Directions

#### A. Temporal Coherence Computing

Instead of maintaining spatial superposition, explore:
- Computation through phase relationships across time
- "Scanning" through solution space like a CRT beam
- Coherence windows rather than coherence preservation

**Testable**: Does this suggest different error correction strategies?

#### B. Resonance-Based Qubit Coupling

Instead of fighting environmental coupling:
- Intentional weak coupling between qubit clusters
- Let them phase-lock naturally (pendulum analogy)
- Computation as orchestrated resonance patterns

**Testable**: Can we design coupling that enhances rather than destroys computation?

#### C. Decoherence as Feature

From Synchronism: coherence (C) and decoherence (1-C) are complementary, not opposed:
- Classical behavior IS the decoherent regime
- Quantum behavior IS the coherent regime
- Computation might live at the **boundary**

**Testable**: Is there an optimal C value for quantum computation, not C→1?

#### D. Intent Dynamics in Quantum Gates

Synchronism derives quantum mechanics from intent conservation:
- ∂I/∂t + ∇·J = 0 (intent conservation)
- iℏ ∂ψ/∂t = Hψ (Schrödinger, derived)

**Question**: What do quantum gates look like in intent-space? Can we design gates that are more "natural" to the underlying dynamics?
