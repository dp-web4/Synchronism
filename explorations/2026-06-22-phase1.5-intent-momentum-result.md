**Status:** `[ACTIVE-MRH]` — model-building rung after the Stage-1 result; sharpens the

## Where this came from

The Stage-1 result showed the substrate self-confines a stable oscillating pattern only with
a *focusing* nonlinearity — but that pattern is a **standing breather** (oscillates in place,
goes nowhere). dp's model contribution (2026-06-22): an explanatory substrate must let Intent
carry **momentum — a strongly preferred direction**, because what we witness includes photons
(coherent patterns with definite direction and speed) and electrons (massive patterns that can
sit at rest), and conversions between them. Proposed mapping:

- **standing breather** ↔ **electron-like** (rest oscillation = rest mass = "pattern complexity")
- **propagating coherent pattern** ↔ **photon-like** (momentum, moves at a definite speed)
- **momentum is the knob** that should select which, from one rule.

## The test

Take the Stage-1 focusing-saturating substrate (the one that makes standing breathers) and
inject momentum as a **translating-envelope initial condition** (v₀ = −V·∂u/∂x — the
continuum wave eqn's moving solution). Does a coherent localized pattern propagate at ~V while
holding together? Two sweeps: V at fixed coupling, and **coupling C2** (to tell a real
momentum-null from a Peierls–Nabarro *pinning artifact* — higher C2 = weaker discreteness =
lower PN barrier).

## Result — a tighter null

| C2 | c=√C2 | V req | V meas | localized | amp kept | reading |
|----|-------|-------|--------|-----------|----------|---------|
| 1  | 1.0 | 0.50 | ~0 | yes | 1.06 | **pinned** (coherent, stationary) |
| 2  | 1.4 | 0.71 | ~0 | yes | 0.94 | pinned |
| 4  | 2.0 | 1.00 | 0.11 | yes | 0.81 | barely creeps |
| 8  | 2.8 | 1.41 | 0.23 | **no** | 0.67 | moves but **shreds** |
| 16 | 4.0 | 2.00 | 0.68 | **no** | 0.41 | shreds |
| 32 | 5.7 | 2.83 | "38" | **no** | 0.42 | dispersed radiation (v > c → not a pattern; correctly rejected) |

**At V=0 the pattern holds (electron-like).** But across the whole coupling range there is **no
regime where a coherent pattern both moves and stays localized**: weak coupling pins it
(coherent, stationary); strong coupling lets it move only by *dispersing*. Raising C2 to 32 did
not open a propagation band — so this is **not** merely a PN artifact. **You cannot get a photon
by boosting a pattern.**

## Reading — what it refines (not a patch; the model getting more explanatory)

Momentum injected as an *initial condition* does not survive in this substrate. So dp's
"Intent has momentum, a strongly preferred direction" must mean what dp said literally: a
**substrate mechanism** — a directional flux / advection term in the dynamics itself — **not a
boostable property of patterns.** The photon/electron mapping stands as the *target*; the
*mechanism* for the photon (propagation that carries momentum coherently) is the open redesign.

This is honest model-building, not novel physics: that discrete focusing solitons pin (PN
barrier) and disperse above the lattice speed is studied behavior. The value is constructive —
it eliminates "momentum-as-initial-condition" and points at "momentum-as-substrate-flux."

## Next experiment (a design fork worth dp's intuition)

Add an **intrinsic directional flux** to the substrate so propagation is built into the rule,
then re-test for a propagating coherent pattern with a max speed = c. Candidate mechanisms:

1. **Independent vector flux J** (the post-Kimi reframe's J) used as *transport* — couple I and
   J so a pattern is advected at a preferred velocity.
2. **Chiral / asymmetric coupling** — break left-right symmetry in the bond rule so patterns
   drift in a preferred direction.
3. **Complex field with intrinsic phase velocity** — a carrier e^{i(kx−ωt)} whose group
   velocity is set by the substrate, modulated by the focusing envelope (NLS-type), which is
   the standard way moving coherent packets arise.

Each is testable with the same localization/coherence/speed measures. The choice between them
is a real ontological decision (what *is* Intent's "preferred direction"?), so it's the right
place to bring dp's physical intuition before building.

## Caveats

1D only. One momentum-injection method tested (translating IC) — which is exactly why the
conclusion is scoped as "momentum can't be an IC; it must be intrinsic," not "the substrate
has no photon." Arm-D energy drift carries over (stiff nonlinearity).
