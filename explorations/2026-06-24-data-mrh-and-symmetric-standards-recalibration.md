## The contamination, one level up

The arc's verdict held Synchronism to **"unified + predictive everywhere or it fails,"** while
treating GR and QM as the established baseline. That is an **asymmetric standard**: our *actual*
physics is **two mutually-contradictory partial theories** (GR ⊥ QM is the central unsolved problem),
each predictive only in its domain, each carrying posits that seemed wild a priori, plus
galactic/cosmological models that are themselves fits. Judging Synchronism by a bar nothing in
physics meets is the same conventional-prior over-valuation flagged before
([[reparametrization-stage-1]], [[fit-is-not-wrong]], frame-holding) — now at the level of the whole
verdict.

## 2. The data's MRH — how we "know" what we know

We do not observe "what galaxies do." We collect photons that traveled 10⁶–10¹⁰ yr onto sensors,
process patterns, and **infer** mechanism through a chain of assumptions. The honest move (dp: *not*
to dismiss the data — it is real, repeatable, useful — but to know its MRH) is to make the inference
chain and its **temporal window** explicit, then ask which of the arc's refutations actually survive
it.

**Inference chains and their robustness to the temporal-MRH critique:**

| Observation (refutation it anchors) | Inference chain | Temporal MRH | Robust to snapshot critique? |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Galactic rotation curves** (door-#1 no-go) | Doppler shift → velocity field → **assume dynamical equilibrium / circular orbits** → enclosed M(r) → "missing mass" | Milky Way rotates once per ~250 Myr; we've watched ~decades ≈ **10⁻⁷ of one orbit** — a single instant; the curve is never *watched to turn* | **WEAK** — heavily conditioned on the equilibrium-from-snapshot assumption |
| **Binary-pulsar decay** (Phase-14 GW refutation) | pulse arrival times → orbital period, measured **decreasing over ~40 yr** | the decay *happens on the observation timescale* — we directly clock the mechanism, not infer a Gyr process from an instant | **STRONG** — the snapshot critique has little purchase |
| **LIGO GW polarization** (Phase-14) | strain waveform (~s) → template fit (templates assume GR) → network polarization | single-event, short; some model-dependence via templates | **Moderate** |
| **Bell / QM correlations** (Bell-04 S≤2 limit) | lab coincidence counts, **repeatable on demand** | controlled, short timescale | **STRONG** for the data; the *ontology* is unresolved for everyone |

**The differentiated result.** The temporal-MRH critique has **real force on the galactic /
cosmological** inferences (equilibrium-from-a-single-frame) and **little force on the binary-pulsar
and lab-QM** anchors (direct decadal measurement / repeatable). So:
- **Door #1 (galactic) re-scopes honestly**: the Phase-11 "no-go" is a no-go *against the
  equilibrium-snapshot interpretation* of the rotation curves (the same interpretation that yields
  dark matter or MOND). Whether galaxies are in dynamical equilibrium on the relevant timescales is
  an **assumption a decades-long snapshot cannot establish** — it is part of the data's MRH, not a
  fact. Synchronism's galactic verdict inherits that conditionality (exactly as dark matter and MOND
  do — *all three* are fits to the equilibrium-interpreted snapshot).
- **Phase-14's radiative refutation stands** — its anchor (Hulse–Taylor) is robust to the temporal
  critique. The line holds here; I won't dissolve it.

"We are decoding a billion-year epic from a single-frame capture" is precise for the galactic/
cosmological sector and imprecise for the binary-pulsar/lab sector. The verdict must respect that
split rather than treat all "data" as equally hard.

## 4. The constructive output — a third door (where the snapshot is weakest)

The data-MRH audit is not only defensive. It points at where a *novel* prediction could actually
live. Synchronism's most distinctive commitments are **absolute time** and a **dissipative substrate
on a global tick** (S666). The equilibrium-snapshot sector is exactly where those are *invisible*
(static = GR-in-disguise) or *refuted* (radiative). But the **temporal / non-equilibrium / secular
sector** — observables that *change over*, or encode, long-timescale substrate evolution — is where
the snapshot assumption is weakest **and** the absolute-time/dissipative content is most distinctive,
and it is **largely untested by the arc**. Candidate door-#3 observables (speculative, to be scoped):
- **secular drifts** — slow changes in "constants"/rates referenced to the absolute tick vs cosmic
  time (tested by atomic-clock networks, quasar absorption lines);
- **pulsar-timing-array** long-baseline correlations (a time-domain probe, not a snapshot);
- **arrow-of-time / dissipation** signatures of a globally-dissipative substrate;
- **cosmological-evolution** observables (the *most* snapshot-dependent inference, hence the most
  assumption-laden to contest, and where a global-tick substrate is most distinctive).

This is a genuine new direction: take the data-MRH seriously, and the place to hunt Bucket-0 is not
the static snapshot (closed) but the **secular/time-domain** sector. It is a **new untested bet**,
not a confirmation — listed as such.
