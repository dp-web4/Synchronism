1. **SUSY is also custodial.** Groot Nibbelink & Pospelov, *PRL* 94, 081601 (2005): exact SUSY forbids dim-3/4 LV operators, forcing LV to dim ≥ 5 (UV-suppressed). So "boost invariance is the *only* custodial symmetry" is wrong. (SUSY breaking reintroduces dim-4 LV suppressed by the breaking scale — so it's a genuine, if not complete, custodian.) A wide hierarchy removes the tuning **perturbatively, without restoring boost invariance and without SUSY.** This directly refutes my "must be non-perturbative / doubly-obstructed" framing: a boost-breaking, perturbative escape exists and is demonstrated for exactly condition (b).

3. **Causal sets** (Bombelli–Lee–Meyer–Sorkin 1987; "discreteness without symmetry breaking"): random Poisson sprinkling is a *discrete* substrate with *no* preferred frame ⇒ no modified dispersion. So the *discrete* class as a whole is not excluded; only the regular-lattice + preferred-frame subclass inherits the problem.
