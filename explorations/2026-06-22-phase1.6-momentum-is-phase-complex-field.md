## The deeper perspective (and the result that confirms it)

**The three are facets of ONE thing: a complex Intent field.** A complex field ψ = √ρ·e^{iφ}
decomposes (Madelung) into ρ = |ψ|² (the **particle/envelope**) and v = ∂ₓφ (a **flux/velocity
field**) with a net tilt (a **preferred direction**). So mechanism 1 (flux J) and mechanism 2
(chirality) are the *phase projections* of mechanism 3. **Momentum lives in the phase.**

Findings:
- **The complex field carries a coherent packet that propagates** at group velocity v_g < c,
  with the lattice signal speed acting as a genuine **ceiling** (held at every k; the k=2 packet
  approaches c and *breaks up / radiates* — physically sensible for a packet pushed toward the
  speed limit). The **real field pins at every k** (no phase to hold momentum) — reproducing
  Phase-1.5 and confirming the diagnosis.
- **Cross-talk is empirical, not asserted:** the Madelung flux J = ∂ₓφ of the complex packet
  **tracks the phase-ramp k** (0.20 → 0.44 → 0.58) with a definite direction. Mechanism 1
  (flux J) and mechanism 2 (preferred direction) literally *live inside* the complex field as
  its phase gradient. The three proposals collapse into one.

## What this means (the model getting more explanatory)

- **The substrate must be complex.** A real Intent field gives you an electron (a standing,
  pinned, focusing-confined envelope) but no photon. A *complex* field gives you both: the
  amplitude envelope is the particle; the phase is the wave that carries momentum.
- **Wave/particle duality is the momentum mechanism, not a paradox.** dp's pointer was exact:
  duality is where classical (real-field) models break, and it breaks there *because* momentum
  must be carried by phase. Photon = phase-dominated propagating packet (low rest oscillation,
  moves at ~c); electron = amplitude-dominated localized envelope (high rest oscillation = mass
  = pattern complexity, sits still). One complex Intent field, momentum (phase-ramp k) as the
  knob between them, c as the ceiling.
- This **realizes dp's intuition** that the real mechanism "has characteristics of all three":
  yes — because all three are the amplitude/phase faces of one complex field.

## Honesty

NOT novel physics: moving complex-Klein-Gordon / NLS solitons, the Madelung hydrodynamic
picture, and the c-ceiling are textbook. The value is **constructive and structural**: it
unifies dp's three proposed mechanisms into one, demonstrates empirically that the substrate
must be complex (real fails, complex succeeds), and grounds the photon/electron split + the
duality framing in a runnable model. It does not (yet) make a novel prediction — the
discipline (PREDICTIONS Bucket 0) is unchanged.

Caveats: 1D; idealized soliton seeding; the k=2 break-up wants a finer-dt check; "focusing"
still breaks Foundation 3's monotonic-saturation axiom (Stage-1 caveat carries over — the
substrate the model is converging on is *complex field + focusing nonlinearity*, not monotonic
saturation).
