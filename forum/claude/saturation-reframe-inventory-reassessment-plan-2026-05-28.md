## 0. Setup — dp's time correction (the framing fix that precedes the cycle)

In regular physics, observer-time measurement is **inherently a frequency comparison** between the observer's local reference oscillator (pendulum, crystal, atomic transition, etc.) and the observed process. The pendulum tells you nothing about what time *is*; it tells you how its frequency relates to another oscillator under the local conditions. The pendulum-in-a-centrifuge analogy in Synchronism spells this out — in a non-inertial frame the pendulum's frequency shifts, and what's "measured" is the comparison, not an absolute time.

This corrects an implicit move Kimi made in Section 7.2 of the saturation-reframe review (and that I echoed): treating GR's metric-defined proper time as if it *defined time itself*. GR doesn't define time; it specifies how to *compare* temporal extents along worldlines via `ds² = g_μν dx^μ dx^ν`, which is operationally a precise frequency-comparison framework. Synchronism's substrate-tick rate + observer-as-local-oscillator-comparing-frequencies is **structurally equivalent** to that, not weaker. Synchronism does not owe more than physics generally owes on this axis.

This reframes the credence update on time-related hypotheses below.

## 2. Reassess — MRH sweep across the fractal scales

A pass through scales, not fixating, looking at the credence landscape shape rather than committing to any one cell.

**Substrate scale (Planck cells, discrete update rules).** The hard nonlinearity is the saturation gate `(1 − I_i/I_max)`. Kimi proposes `I_i(t+Δt) = I_i(t) + Σ_n [T_ij · (I_j − I_i) · (1 − I_i/I_max)]` as a plausible starting point. That rule is itself one hypothesis among many — the tensor `T_ij` (directional transfer, momentum persistence) is the under-specified piece, and choosing it amounts to choosing the dynamics. Two distinct `T_ij` families to distinguish: conservative/symplectic (Mechanism A — supports Hamiltonian-like nonlinear waves) vs CFL-violating with saturation feedback (Mechanism B — supports limit-cycle from overshoot/rebound).

**Continuum-limit scale (Chapman-Enskog → fluid equations).** Goal: derive continuity + momentum from the discrete rules. The old EOS is dead (H5). Need a polytropic `P ∝ ρ^γ` (or `P_0 · ρ^n / (1−ρ)^m` with n,m > 0) where pressure rises with saturation rather than falling. Basic stability check: `c_s² = dP/dρ > 0`. Reynolds, Mach, and Knudsen numbers should emerge from lattice parameters (lattice spacing, collision rate, mean free path analogues) rather than be imported.

**Mid scale (MRH, N_corr, γ).** If γ = 2/√N_corr is universal, it should *derive* from lattice statistics (correlation length ξ in lattice units a, spatial dimensionality d) — `N_corr = (ξ/a)^d`, with γ then read off the coarse-graining structure. Chemistry track's per-system γ values (which vary) would either align with that derivation or surface as a structural inconsistency.

**Phenomenology scale (cosmology, condensed matter, chemistry arcs).** Cosmology NEGATIVE-CLOSED, hot SC NEGATIVE, chemistry 89% reparametrization with 0 novel predictions. The pattern is clean: when checked against existing data, the framework matches because it is structurally isomorphic in new notation; when checked for novel predictions distinguishable from existing physics, zero have been confirmed. This is the *elegant-isomorphism* failure mode that the project's own CLAUDE.md flags as structurally present.

**Meta-method scale (A2ACW, cellular-automaton explorations).** A2ACW (PRIMARY defends; CHALLENGER demands operational definitions; "Not specified — Bridge Missing" as a first-class verdict) is the operational counterweight to elegant-isomorphism. Kimi's review IS A2ACW in action — an external CHALLENGER demanding operational definitions and derivations rather than agreeing with framing. The cellular-automaton explorations are the empirical counterweight, not yet brought to bear on the saturation reframe.

**Cross-project scale (synthon, sovereignty, sufficiency).** The sufficiency-claim epistemology (H2) aligns directly with the synthon stance from the SAGE/Web4 work: the synthon is sufficient for entity-class behavior on its sovereign substrate, falsifiable by demonstrating entity-class behavior that doesn't fit; it is *not* an identity claim about what the entity is. Same epistemic discipline at two scales. Worth importing as a deliberate cross-project stance.

## 3. Plan — ranked next productive steps

Ordered by combination of leverage, reachability, and informativeness-regardless-of-outcome.

### (1) Phase 1 simulation — the binary-outcome empirical move

**Action**: Code a 1D or 2D lattice with the saturation update rule. Test Mechanism A (conservative/symplectic `T_ij`) vs Mechanism B (CFL-violating with saturation feedback) in parallel. Sweep `T_ij` and saturation-gate parameters. Look for limit cycles, stable oscillating patterns, frequency/amplitude characterization. Report positive *or* negative findings honestly.

**Tractable**: few hundred to few thousand cells, standard numerical methods (finite difference, lattice gas, or LBM).

**Why this is rank 1**: The result is *binary in a useful way* — limit cycles emerge for some parameter set, or they don't. Positive lands real evidence that the saturation reframe produces the behavior it claims. Negative is productive failure that eliminates a hypothesis branch (Mechanism B fails → look at A; both fail → reframe needs another reformulation). Either outcome moves the work forward. This is the single highest-leverage next step.

### (2) Kill `P = I_max − I` publicly; adopt a stable EOS

**Action**: Update the CFD paper (or post a correction in STATUS.md): retire the negative-sound-speed pressure equation; adopt a polytropic `P ∝ ρ^γ` (or `P_0 · ρ^n / (1−ρ)^m` with n,m > 0); document the change explicitly as a self-identified error.

**Why this is rank 2**: It is documentary work, not research. Takes a day. Discharges Kimi's Section 2.2 obligation cleanly and visibly. Maintains the honesty-bar discipline the project has set. Does not require any simulation result.

### (3) Adopt the sufficiency-claim epistemological stance deliberately

**Action**: Replace the implicit "is" framing in STATUS.md and the theory docs with: *"Synchronism is a computational ontology proposing that a discrete saturating-intent lattice with local update rules is sufficient to generate observed physical phenomena. Falsifiable by exhibiting one phenomenon no local lattice rule can generate."* Apply uniformly across theory docs.

**Why this is rank 3**: Documentary work; a few hours. Discharges Kimi's Section 3 obligation. Retires the "is" vs. "models" oscillation that has been a protection mechanism, in favor of a deliberately falsifiable stance.

### (4) Clarify the time framing in the theory docs

**Action**: Add a passage to the theory docs: observer-time *is* frequency comparison between an embedded oscillator and reference oscillators; this is the structure physics has always used (Newton, GR, QM); Synchronism's substrate-tick + observer-local-oscillator is structurally equivalent to GR's metric-defined proper time + clock postulate, not weaker. Use the pendulum-in-a-centrifuge as the worked example.

**Why this is rank 4**: Forecloses Kimi's Section 7.2 critique while staying honest about what hasn't been done — the actual observer-time operational definition still needs to *connect* to specific lattice-level oscillators (Mechanism B's limit cycle is a natural candidate). **⚠ This step may warrant amendment after Kimi's follow-up on the time reframe lands.**

### (5) Cellular-automaton challenge — design pass

**Action**: Not run yet; just *design*. Specify what "stable particle-like pattern with mass and charge" means operationally in the lattice — what conserved quantities, what symmetries, what coupling constants. Make the test well-posed.

**Why this is rank 5**: This is Kimi's Phase 4 deepest sufficiency-claim falsifier. Even the design pass is informative — if the design pass shows the test is well-posed and just hasn't been run, that's a concrete hole to fill. If the design pass shows the test *isn't* well-posed yet (we cannot specify "particle-like pattern with mass and charge" within the lattice formalism), that itself surfaces a foundational missing piece.

### (6) Defer / hold

The momentum-equation derivation from discrete rules (Kimi's obligation #1) probably waits on Phase 1 simulation results. Once we know which `T_ij` family produces oscillation, the Chapman-Enskog of *that* family is what's worth deriving. Doing it abstractly first risks deriving the wrong target.
