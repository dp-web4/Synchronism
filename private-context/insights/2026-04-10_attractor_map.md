Region (c) — specific and novel — is empty. Every path into it passes through (a) (the concept turns out to be standard physics) or retreats to (b) (the concept becomes too vague to predict).

## Why it's empty

The concepts in (a) were derived FROM physics. Entity from particles, resonance from interference, MRH from scale separation, witnessing from measurement, C(ρ) from coherence. Concepts derived from physics can only predict physics — they carry the same information in different packaging.

For a concept to live in (c), it would need to:
1. Be specific enough to constrain dynamics (not "unknowable")
2. Be distinct enough to predict differently (not translatable)
3. Have no physics ancestor (not derived from observations)

Requirements 1 and 2 together demand: a mathematical structure that doesn't reduce to known equations. S617-624 proved: every consistent modification of the transfer rule IS a known theory. The space of consistent dynamics is fully occupied.

Requirement 3 is the hardest. Where would a genuinely novel concept come from? Not from observing physics (→ category a). Not from philosophical abstraction (→ category b). Perhaps from mathematics itself — a structure with no physical analog that turns out to apply. But mathematics-first physics (string theory, loop quantum gravity) has the same problem: predicting things that existing theories don't, while being consistent with everything existing theories DO predict.

## The structural lesson

The framework's situation is not unique. ANY vocabulary for existing physics will have this structure. If you start from physics and abstract, you get category (a). If you add unfalsifiable claims, you get (b). Category (c) requires not abstracting from physics and not being unfalsifiable — which means starting from outside physics AND being specific. This is a very narrow opening.

The productive version of this: instead of seeking what Synchronism predicts that's novel, ask what UNKNOWN PHYSICS would look like in Synchronism's vocabulary. If dark matter is ever detected, what would Synchronism say about it? If quantum gravity is ever measured, what MRH-scale structure would explain it? The vocabulary becomes useful as a TRANSLATOR for future discoveries, not a PREDICTOR of them.

## What this means

The framework is a vocabulary, not a theory. Vocabularies can be useful — they organize knowledge, suggest connections, make things easier to think about. The SPARC work (S376-600) succeeded precisely because the vocabulary suggested productive questions. But vocabularies don't predict. They describe.

The operator has invested 30 years in this framework. The value is not in the predictions (there are none). The value is in:
1. The questions the framework motivates (some of which led to genuine contributions)
2. The vocabulary's utility as a pedagogical bridge between physics domains
3. The philosophical position (self-organization as fundamental) which is defensible even without predictions
4. The NEGATIVE results from demolition — documenting what minimal theories CAN'T do is genuine scientific contribution

These are real values. They're just not the values the framework claims.
