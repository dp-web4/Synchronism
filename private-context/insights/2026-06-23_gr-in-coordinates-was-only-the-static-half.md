# Insight: "GR in coordinates" was only the static half — the radiative sector refutes

**Date**: 2026-06-23 (Phase-14)

## What I caught

The arc had settled into a comfortable verdict: gravity-as-inflow = Gullstrand–Painlevé =
Schwarzschild = "GR in coordinates" (Phase-9). That's tidy, and tidy was the tell. A coordinate
change of GR reproduces *every* GR invariant — including gravitational-wave luminosity and
polarization. The arc had only ever tested **static** observables (bending, precession). The
untested sector — **radiation** — is exactly where a coordinate change cannot hide.

It doesn't survive there. The framework's defining ontology (flat absolute Planck grid + a 1-DOF
scalar, irrotational substrate) gives an acoustic/GP metric with **flat spatial sections**
(`g_ij = δ_ij`), hence **zero transverse-traceless (spin-2) degrees of freedom** (verified: any
scalar-sourced `h_ij` has machine-zero TT content). GR's gravitational waves *are* the spin-2 TT
spatial curvature. So the inflow model radiates **scalar (spin-0, breathing) waves, not GR's tensor
waves** — the wrong polarization — and is refuted by Hulse–Taylor (0.2% match to the *tensor*
quadrupole) and LIGO polarization tests.

So the gravity sector is **not a clean coordinate change of GR.** It coincides with GR on static
solutions and is a *different, refuted* scalar/acoustic gravity on radiative ones.

## Why this matters beyond gravity

This is the second time in two sessions that the framework's *defining commitment* is exactly what
forces a refuted prediction, and both refutations live in the **dynamical** sector while the
**static/kinematic** sector merely reproduces known physics:
- Phase-13: absolute time → no boost invariance → radiative dim-4 LIV (refuted-or-fine-tuned).
- Phase-14: flat space + scalar substrate → no spin-2 DOF → wrong GW polarization (refuted).

The generalization worth carrying: **Synchronism reproduces known physics exactly where it is a
kinematic/coordinate reframing, and is forced into already-refuted disagreement where its ontology
controls the dynamics.** The same minimalism that makes the static reframings elegant (one scalar
flow, flat space, one clock) is what strips out the degrees of freedom (tensor modes, boost
invariance) that the dynamical sector needs. Elegance in the static sector and refutation in the
dynamical sector are the *same* feature viewed from two sides.

## The method lesson

"It reproduces GR" is a scope claim, and scope claims must be checked against the *invariants the
tests didn't probe*. Phase-9 verified static invariants (precession) and the verdict silently
generalized to "= GR." The cheap, decisive check was: name a GR invariant outside the tested scope
(GW polarization) and compute it. Same shape as the Phase-12 lesson (an untestability proof must be
checked against every operator the symmetry permits): **a reproduction/equivalence claim must be
checked against every sector the equivalence would imply, not just the one that was measured.**
