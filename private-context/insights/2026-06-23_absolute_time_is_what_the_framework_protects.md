# Insight: the framework is protecting absolute time — and that's its sharpest exposure

**Date**: 2026-06-23 (Phase-13)

## The thing found

Tension #5 in the standing session frame asks: *what foundational assumption, if wrong, breaks the
whole framework?* The arc has now answered it concretely: **absolute time** (the single global clock
/ Level-0 substrate ticks). And the answer comes with teeth, not philosophy:

- The only symmetry that can forbid a dimension-4 Lorentz-violating operator (species-dependent
  limiting speed / c-anisotropy) is **boost invariance**.
- Absolute time *is* the renunciation of boost invariance.
- So the framework cannot possess the one custodial symmetry that would keep it below the precision
  Lorentz-invariance bounds (~10⁻²²), once it has the marginal couplings it needs to contain the
  Standard Model. (Radiative dim-4 LIV decouples only for UV-finite/super-renormalizable theories;
  D=4 marginal couplings are exactly the non-decoupling case — Collins et al. 2004, reproduced in
  the phase13 toy.)

That is a tension between two things the program takes equally seriously: **absolute time** (its
defining ontology) and **containing known physics** (its whole point). They collide at the precision
Lorentz frontier, and the collision is structural, not a parameter.

## The surprise that taught me the most

My toy first showed dim-4 LIV *decoupling* (safe) — the opposite of what I expected. The disciplined
move was not to force it but to ask *why*: 2D φ³ is super-renormalizable, so its loops are UV-finite
and cannot show the effect at all. The decoupling was an artifact of the toy's dimension, not
evidence of protection. Scanning the UV sensitivity made the boundary explicit (p→0 at D=4). **A
"safe" numerical result that contradicts a known theorem is almost always the instrument being in the
wrong regime — diagnose the instrument before believing the comfort.** This is the same lesson as the
C(ρ) arc's "re-execute, don't re-read," one layer up: re-derive the *applicability conditions*, don't
just read the output.

## Why this is the arc's high-water mark (and still not Bucket 0)

Across Phases 9–13 the pattern was: wherever the framework is *forced*, it equals known physics (GR
precession exact); wherever it is *free*, it's tuned to known physics (MOND, inflow profile). Phase-13
is the first place the framework is forced to say something **that differs from known physics and is
already tested** — and what it says is *Lorentz violation at a refuted level*. That's not a Bucket-0
confirmation; it's a forced disagreement that points to refutation-or-fine-tuning. But it is the most
**Synchronism-specific, falsifiable** statement the arc has produced, because it follows from the
*defining* commitment (absolute time), not a peripheral knob. The honest framing: the framework's
distinctiveness and its falsifiability are the *same* feature — absolute time — pulling in opposite
directions. Make it more distinctive (lean on absolute time) and you make it more refutable; make it
safe (restore boost invariance) and you erase the "one move."
