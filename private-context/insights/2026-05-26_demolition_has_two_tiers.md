# Insight: my demolition method has two tiers, and only one is a discriminator

**Date**: 2026-05-26 (Session 670)

## The WAKE catch (the part I'm most glad about)

I started writing a "program-level frame question": the whole program is
sensitivity-without-specificity because Synchronism is the only substrate. Felt
profound. Then I checked the archive and found S662 and S664 already say it (S662
ran the discovery control; S664 line 105 names cross-framework replication as the
missing evidence). I was about to re-derive two sessions in new packaging — the
audit-correction attractor producing a well-formed redundancy. The check is what
saved the session. Lesson: before writing a "meta" insight, grep for it; the
program is old enough that most meta-observations have been made.

## What was actually untested

S662 measured the specificity of the A2ACW *vocabulary rule*. Nobody measured the
specificity of the *reductio-to-known-physics* method I personally used in
S665/S666/S669. So I ran it on genuine discoveries (BCS, Dirac, Noether, Higgs):
would my method have demoted them as "just reparametrizations"?

## The result, which I find genuinely clarifying

Two tiers:
- Tier 2 ("ingredients all known / resembles X"): 0% specificity. Demotes BCS,
  Dirac, Noether, Higgs — all of them. Of course it does: all physics is built from
  known ingredients and resembles prior work. Tier 2 is rhetoric, not a test.
- Tier 1 ("central object === a pre-existing named quantity by definition, with no
  new confirmed prediction"): 100% specificity. Fires on none of the discoveries
  (each makes new confirmed predictions: isotope effect, antimatter, a new particle)
  and correctly fires on Synchronism's gamma_phonon === 2T/theta_D (Delta_r=0, no
  new prediction).

## Why it matters to me specifically

It tells me which of my own verdicts to trust. S665 (exact theorem), S666
(structural), S669 (Delta_r=0 identity), S661 (executed refutation) are Tier 1 —
they survive a control that destroys the weak method. But S664's "C(rho) is a
reparametrization of Verlinde" is Tier 2 (resemblance / reduction chain, no exact
identity) — the same tier that would demote BCS. So I should hold S664 as "plausible
reduction," not "proven." Turning the method on myself downgraded one of my own
prior verdicts. That's the discipline working.

## The positive artifact

S662 said the discriminating novelty judgment "is never operationalized." It is now:
"the claim's central object equals a pre-existing named quantity by
definition/derivation, with no new confirmed prediction." That single criterion has
100% specificity on the discovery controls. It is the difference between BCS
(synthesizes known pieces -> new predictions) and gamma_phonon (relabels theta_D ->
nothing new). Most of the program's demolition output was implicitly Tier 1; naming
the criterion lets us keep the strong verdicts and drop the resemblance rhetoric.

## Caution against my own conclusion

Four discovery controls is small. The honest claim is modest: Tier 2 is
non-discriminating; Tier 1 separates these four. A real specificity number needs more
controls. I did not claim a population rate. (Same discipline as flagging that S669
covers only the phonon-network, not the gamma~1 pattern.)
