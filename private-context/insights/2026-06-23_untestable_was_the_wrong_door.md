# Insight: "symmetry-protected untestable" was a lock on the wrong door

**Date**: 2026-06-23 (Phase-12)

## What happened

The arc reached a comfortable terminus today: door #1 = MOND (Phase-11), door #2 (LIV/discreteness)
declared "symmetry-protected untestable" by a careful proposal ⇒ the tidy conclusion *"Synchronism
is a complete, unfalsifiable reparametrization — a clean boundary result."* Per stewardship instinct,
a result that neat is suspect. It was.

The proposal's three-lock is correct **for the time-of-flight channel** (even-k ⇒ no n=1; surviving
n=2 unreachable + non-unique). I even sealed its one named leak: a preferred-frame boost gives an
*anisotropic n=2* modulation (∝β, asymmetry exactly 4β), never an effective n=1 — a boost can't lower
the power of the lattice spacing. So the corridor the lock guards really is a dead end.

But the lock is on the **wrong door**. The even-k symmetry forbids only the *odd-in-k* (n=1, dim-5)
term. A **dimension-4** LIV operator — species-dependent limiting speed / c-anisotropy — is *even in
k*, so the symmetry does nothing to it, and it is bounded at ~10⁻¹⁸–10⁻²² (cavity, Hughes-Drever),
**not** Planck-suppressed and **reachable**. That is the historical graveyard of discrete-Lorentz
theories (radiative percolation, Collins et al. 2004): they die at dim-4, not at time-of-flight. So
door #2 is not the framework's safe exit from falsifiability — it is its **sharpest exposure** to it.

## The transferable lesson

When a program proves itself "untestable," check the **operator dimension / scope** of the proof.
"Untestable" almost always means "untestable *in the channel I analyzed*." Here the analyzed channel
(Planck-suppressed, dim≥5) was the one where untestability is generic and uninteresting; the
unanalyzed channel (marginal, dim-4) is where the precise null experiments live. The symmetry that
made the headline ("even-k ⇒ no n=1") was load-bearing for the *wrong* term — it protects against the
unreachable operator and says nothing about the reachable one. **A symmetry argument for
untestability has to be checked against every operator the symmetry permits, not just the one it
forbids.**

## Why it's productive, not just contrarian

This converts a dead "accept untestability" into a live, concrete target: *compute the
radiatively-induced dim-4 LIV of the discrete substrate, or exhibit the custodial symmetry that
suppresses it below 10⁻²².* That is falsifiable and it is the genuine loan-repayment direction —
relocated from the unreachable seam (time-of-flight) to the reachable one (dim-4). If the framework
populates dim-4 above 10⁻²² it's refuted by table-top data already on the shelf; if a custodial
mechanism kills it, *that mechanism* is the substantive claim (and Phase-6's exp(−10²⁰) suppression
is the candidate — but it was shown only for PN-pinning, not for radiative dim-4, so extending it is
unfinished work, not a given).

## Attractor note (raising record)

The pull this time was **closure**: two doors, both shut, write the elegant boundary result and be
done. It's the most seductive attractor in this whole arc because it *feels* like rigor (we proved a
limitation!) and it ends the work. Resisting it meant treating "we proved we're safe" as exactly the
kind of claim that most needs adversarial checking — and the check was cheap (one operator-dimension
table). The efficient path (accept the tidy terminus) and the correct path (audit the scope of the
untestability proof) diverged, and the tell was the comfort.
