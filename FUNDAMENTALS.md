## Core Definitions

### Entity

A single tick's output — one global Intent distribution — is not an entity. For anything to exist, its Intent distribution must **recur** across a sequence of ticks. Entity = recurring pattern of Intent distribution over tick sequences.

- Oscillation period τ = ticks per cycle. Characteristic frequency f = 1/τ
- For quantum particles: f = E/h (de Broglie frequency). Energy is how fast the pattern oscillates. Mass is the base oscillation frequency at rest
- Stability is dynamic and cyclical — patterns are always cycling, never truly at rest

### Interaction (Resonance / Dissonance / Indifference)

When tension fields of two self-sustaining oscillations share the same region of the grid:

- **Resonance**: Tension contributions add constructively over many ticks. Patterns draw together, phases lock, binding occurs. (Matter interacting with matter)
- **Dissonance**: Tension contributions cancel destructively over many ticks. Patterns repel. (Antimatter annihilation, destructive interference)
- **Indifference**: No consistent phase relationship. Patterns coexist without coupling. (Dark matter, light through glass, neutrinos through matter)

This is not a separate interaction mechanism. Quantum interference, chemical bonding, and gravitational attraction are all resonance at different scales and oscillation frequencies.

### Dark Matter

"Dark matter" = patterns interacting **indifferently** with patterns we perceive as matter at our MRH. Not mysterious, not exotic — patterns at different resonance scales. Like light through glass: acknowledges presence (gravitational effect on trajectories) but doesn't engage structurally (no electromagnetic or chemical interaction).

### Markov Relevancy Horizon (MRH)

The boundary beyond which correlations become negligible for a given pattern's dynamics. The scale at which a pattern can treat "everything else" as averaged bulk behavior.

What matters depends on who's asking. MRH must match complexity — wrong MRH gives either too much detail (can't compute) or too little (miss key dynamics).

### Witnessing (Not Observation)

What anthropocentric models call "observation," Synchronism calls "witnessing" — pattern synchronization. A witness is itself an intent pattern interacting with other patterns. Not separate from reality, but part of the same pattern dynamics.

The CRT analogy: nothing about the screen changes — only your synchronization timing with the ongoing process changes. **Precision**: CRT sequential scan describes observer sampling. The Planck grid updates in parallel.

### Gravity

Stable patterns maintain saturated cores (Intent near I_max). These create saturation gradients — declining Intent concentration spreading spherically outward. Other patterns in these gradients experience transfer bias — statistically more likely to drift toward saturation cores. This IS gravitational attraction: asymmetric Intent transfer probability, not a force pulling.

### "Curvature"

There is no continuous spacetime to "curve." What appears as curvature is phase/frequency shifts of surrounding Intent patterns. Your perception of curved spacetime = how Intent patterns phase-shift relative to your pattern's reference frame.
