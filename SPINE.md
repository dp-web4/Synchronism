## Two load-bearing pieces: Intent (a reification) and saturation (the wall-former)

The move above rests on two pieces that are easy to miss and easy to misread. Both are
**fundamental**, not decoration.

**1. Intent is a reification, not an ontology.** Intent is a *computational abstraction* — a
useful fiction like π, or like "the wavefunction" — that makes an underlying "greater force"
tractable to simulate. It is **not** a force, **not** anthropocentric will, **not** a claim
about what reality ultimately *is*. Demanding SI units for Intent is the same category error
as demanding them for π. What the model commits to is the *dynamics*, not the ontological
reality of the variable. (FUNDAMENTALS §2.)

**2. Saturation is what builds the walls.** Without saturation resistance, Intent just
dissipates down every gradient — no patterns, no entities, uniform noise. Saturation
(resistance rising as a region fills toward its limit, `R(I) = [1 − (I/I_max)^n]`) is **the**
mechanism that lets a pattern hold together: a filling region resists further inflow, forming
the **wall** that stabilizes a standing oscillation. Every entity — particle to galaxy —
exists because saturation built it a boundary. (FUNDAMENTALS §3.)

### Saturation makes emergence a *step function*, not a hockey stick

This is the most-misread consequence, and it is a reading guide for the whole program.
Emergence and phase change are **steps**, and a step has three parts:

- **The flat floor.** Before the threshold, almost nothing moves. *This can look like
  refutation* — "no signal, it doesn't work." It isn't; it's the pre-step regime.
- **The step.** Past the threshold the change is steep and fast. *This can look like
  unstable, unbounded growth* — a runaway hockey stick. It isn't; it's bounded.
- **The ceiling.** Saturation arrives, the rise plateaus — **and the ceiling becomes the
  next floor.** A new stable baseline, from which a later step can rise again.

Floor → step → ceiling-that-becomes-the-next-floor is the *signature* of saturation, and it
is everywhere: phase transitions, emergence, the program's own Coupling-Coherence result (a
Hill/**step** function beat a smooth tanh by ΔAIC = 4 — the data preferred the step), and
well outside physics — model capability vs. parameter count (flat, then a sudden jump at a
scale threshold, then a plateau that becomes the new floor).

**Why it matters for reading the ledger:** a **bounded plateau is the mechanism working,
not failing.** (The CHSH result below is a clean case — the local single-observer
construction *saturates* at S = 2, the classical ceiling, and cannot pass it: a saturation
wall, drawn honestly.) So in [PREDICTIONS.md](PREDICTIONS.md), keep two things apart that a
flat curve can blur: a genuine **refutation** (tested against data, failed, eliminated —
those stay refuted) versus a **flat floor** mistaken for one. The step-function lens is a
reading guide, not a license to un-refute — but "nothing yet" and "it failed" are different
claims, and saturation is why.

## Why anyone should care: the geocentric → heliocentric wager

For centuries, astronomers explained planetary motion by adding epicycles to a model with
**Earth at the center**. The math worked. Predictions improved. The model grew more
complex and more accurate — and was fundamentally wrong. Heliocentrism didn't refine the
epicycles; it made them vanish, by removing the assumption that put the observer at the
center.

**The wager:** modern physics may place *the observer* at the center the same way Ptolemy
placed *the Earth* there.

- Quantum measurement "collapses" the wavefunction — around an observer.
- Simultaneity is observer-dependent.
- The observer is treated as separate from the observed; consciousness is privileged.

Synchronism asks: **what if the paradoxes are epicycles?** What if they are artifacts of
the observer-centric frame, not features of nature — and removing that assumption makes
them simpler?

This is a **falsifiable bet on a paradigm**, not a proof. It earns its keep only if the
heliocentric frame eventually predicts something the geocentric frame doesn't (see
[PREDICTIONS.md](PREDICTIONS.md) — that ledger currently reads *zero confirmed*, with a
short list of untested-but-falsifiable bets). The point of stating it this clearly is so
the bet can be **tested and lost in the open**, not so it can be believed.

*Status: a research-direction wager. Compelling as a frame; unproven as physics. The
honest job is to find the test that distinguishes it from standard QM/GR — see "the one
test that matters," below.*

## The two analogies that carry the move

These are the project's geocentric→heliocentric vehicles. They are **intuition pumps**,
not derivations.

### CRT: measurement is synchronization, not observation

An old cathode-ray-tube screen draws its image by sweeping a single electron beam across
phosphor, dot by dot. The whole image is *never* simultaneously present — it is a temporal
average constructed by the viewer's integration window.

- Watch at ~30 Hz: a stable, complete picture.
- Watch at millisecond timing: it breaks into scanning bands.
- Watch at pixel-duration timing: a single dot moving "unpredictably."

**Nothing about the screen changed — only your synchronization timing changed.**

The claim: "wave-particle duality" is an artifact of *when* you synchronize with an
unchanging cycling process, not a property of the process. Superposition is *incomplete
synchronization* with a pattern cycling faster than your sample rate. Entanglement is
*correlated cycles holding coherence*, not spooky action. (Note the precision in
FUNDAMENTALS §Witnessing: the CRT's *sequential* scan models the **observer's sampling**;
the Planck grid itself updates in **parallel**.)

### Pendulum clock: relativistic effects may be instrument effects

Flying atomic clocks diverge by exactly the predicted relativistic amount — famous
confirmation. Now spin a *pendulum* clock in a centrifuge and compare it to a stationary
one. It too diverges by a readily predictable amount. Does that prove "centrifuge time
dilation"? No — it proves the variable we control (centrifugal force) predictably affects
**the instrument** we use to measure time.

The question: what if relativistic effects tell us about our **measurement instruments**
(atoms, photons, rulers, clocks — all patterns) rather than about "spacetime itself"?
This motivates the **two-level time** ontology: Level-0 absolute substrate ticks (Planck
frequency) vs. Level-1+ pattern-relative frequency comparison (all *observed* time).

*Status: both analogies are vivid and load-bearing for the intuition. Neither is a
derivation. They tell you what the model is **claiming**; they do not establish it. The
work of turning "synchronization timing" into a quantitative, distinguishing prediction is
the open frontier (and is exactly what the observer-relative measurement test, below,
attacks).*

## The one test that matters

A standard coupled-oscillator or digital-physics model is *observer-free* — computed from
a God's-eye view. The single thing that makes Synchronism **structurally different** is the
single-observer constraint: **there is no God's-eye view; all measurement is what one
pattern can reconstruct of another via their mutual phase-lock.**

That constraint is also the only place a **novel, distinguishing prediction** can live. The
sharp form of the question:

> Can a purely **local** substrate, measured **only** through an observer-pattern's
> phase-lock to a target-pattern (with freely chosen measurement settings, à la CHSH),
> reproduce the **quantum** correlations — *without* superluminal signaling?

- If **yes** — Synchronism has a genuine physics result (local single-observer dynamics
  reproducing Bell-inequality violation is a major claim).
- If **no** (the likely outcome — local hidden-variable-like substrates generally can't
  violate Bell without superdeterminism) — that is a **clean, productive failure**: it
  sharpens that Synchronism is an **ontology / interpretation**, not a local-hidden-variable
  *physics*, and it tells us exactly what the frame can and cannot claim.

This test is now a concrete, runnable harness:
[`simulations/kuramoto-lattice-suite/`](simulations/kuramoto-lattice-suite/) (the
observer-relative CHSH experiment). It connects to the standing open question
[OQ006 — Measurement Framework Integration](Research/OPEN_QUESTION_Measurement_Framework_Integration.md).

*Status: **run complete, both arms (2026-06-21).** (a) The faithful **local** single-observer
construction gives CHSH **S = 1.98 ≤ 2** (no signaling). (b) The **nonlocal-grid** variant —
the "one substrate" mediating across separated regions — gives **S ≡ 2.0 for all coupling
strengths**, with no signaling, because smooth grid-mixing turns out to be **gauge-equivalent
to relabeling the measurement angles** (it stays local-realist and buys nothing). The
