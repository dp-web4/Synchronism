### The Problem

Standard quantum mechanics says:
- Without measurement: Schrödinger equation (deterministic evolution)
- With measurement: Wave function collapse (probabilistic outcome)

**The question**: What distinguishes "measurement" from other interactions?

### The Coherence Answer

**Measurement IS coherence projection.**

When a quantum system interacts with an observer:
1. System has coherence distributed across states
2. Observer has its own coherence basis
3. Interaction projects system coherence onto observer's basis
4. "Collapse" = concentration of coherence in projected state

### Mathematical Structure

```
Pre-measurement:
  System coherence: Σᵢ cᵢ = 1 (distributed)
  Entropy: S = -Σ cᵢ log(cᵢ) > 0

Measurement (projection):
  Observer coherence C_obs filters states
  Projection efficiency ∝ C_obs × coupling

Post-measurement:
  Coherence concentrated: c_measured ≈ 1
  Entropy: S → 0
```

### Key Insight

```
'Wave function collapse' IS coherence projection.
The observer doesn't 'cause' collapse - it's a coherence filter.
Observer's coherence basis determines measurement outcome.
No mystical role for consciousness - just coherence dynamics.
```
