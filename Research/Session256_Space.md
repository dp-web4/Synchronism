## Part 2: Coherence Distance

### The Fundamental Equation

```
d(A,B) = -log(C_AB / √(C_A × C_B))
```

Where:
- **C_AB** = joint coherence between A and B
- **C_A, C_B** = individual coherences
- The ratio measures correlation above independence

### Properties

| Property | Proof |
|----------|-------|
| d(A,A) = 0 | C_AA = C_A → ratio = 1 → log(1) = 0 |
| d(A,B) = d(B,A) | Symmetric in A,B |
| Triangle inequality | From correlation properties |
| d > 0 when C_AB < √(C_A×C_B) | Log of ratio < 1 |

### Interpretation

Two systems are "close" if they share coherence beyond independence.

**Example**:
- Entangled particles: C_AB >> √(C_A×C_B) → very close
- Independent systems: C_AB = √(C_A×C_B) → infinite distance
- Nearby objects: High C_AB → short distance
