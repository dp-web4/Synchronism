S622 proposed: "A universe with both gravitational structure formation AND accelerating expansion requires at least two irreducible dynamical ingredients."

The chemistry track provides independent empirical evidence for this at the material scale:

### Channel independence as irreducibility

| Channel pair | Correlation | Interpretation |

| γ_phonon vs γ_electron | weak (~0.15) | Lattice vibrations ≠ electronic transport |
| γ_phonon vs γ_spin | none | Lattice ≠ magnetic ordering |
| γ_phonon vs γ_optical | r = 0.158 | Lattice ≠ electronic transitions |
| γ_electron vs γ_spin | ~0.7 (d-band confound) | Correlated through shared electrons, not through γ |

One parameter (θ_D / γ_phonon) cannot describe all properties of even a single crystal. You need at least:
- θ_D for phonon properties (thermal conductivity, specific heat)
- Band structure for electronic properties (conductivity, Hall effect)
- SOC coupling ξ for magnetic properties (anisotropy, magnetostriction)
- Surface energetics for boundary properties (thermionic emission)

**The condensed matter analog of "one ingredient is insufficient"**: just as one scalar field can't produce both gravity and dark energy (S622), one coherence parameter can't produce both thermal and magnetic behavior. The irreducibility is structural — different physics requires different parameters.

### The SOC dominance parameter as a regime boundary

D = ξ_SOC / (k_B × θ_D) separates regimes where lattice coherence matters (D < 5) from regimes where atomic physics dominates (D > 5). This is a practical expression of the minimum complexity theorem: below D = 5, you might approximate with one ingredient; above D = 5, you provably cannot.
