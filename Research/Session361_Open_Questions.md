## Verification Tests

### Test 1: The Origin of the Constant "2" ✓

**Question**: Why γ = 2/√N and not 1/√N or π/√N?

| Interpretation | Explanation | Plausibility | Weakness |
|----------------|-------------|--------------|----------|
| Statistical | Factor of 2 from variance vs std dev | 80% | Why variance = 4/N? |
| Geometric | Diameter vs radius of phase circle | 70% | Connection is ad hoc |
| **Quantum** | **Heisenberg uncertainty ℏ/2** | **90%** | **Why ℏ/2 → 2/√N?** |
| Dimensional | Real + imaginary components | 60% | Doesn't explain form |
| Combinatorial | Binary choices, spin-1/2 | 75% | Not derived rigorously |

**Status**: The "2" is empirically well-supported but not derived from first principles. Most promising: connection to Heisenberg uncertainty ℏ/2.

### Test 2: Why Phase, Not Something Else? ✓

**Question**: Why is phase the fundamental quantity? Could there be something deeper?

| Property | Necessity | Why Special |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Interference | 99% | Quantum superposition emerges naturally |
| Topological | 98% | Discrete charges from continuous substrate |
| Periodicity | 95% | Natural discretization without boundaries |
| Continuity | 90% | Continuous dynamics possible |
| Gauge invariance | 85% | Only relations matter |
| Compactness | 80% | No infinities, natural regularization |

**Alternative candidates**:
- Amplitude: Cannot work (positive definite, no interference)
- Real scalar: Cannot work (no periodicity, no topological charges)
- Vector: Could work, but phase is the essential part
- Spinor: Could work, might be more fundamental, but still has phase

**Status**: Phase appears uniquely suited (91% average necessity), but we cannot prove nothing deeper exists.
