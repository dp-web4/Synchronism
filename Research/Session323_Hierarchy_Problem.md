## Key Results (8/8 verified)

### Part 1: The Hierarchy Problem

**The Numbers**:
```
M_EW (Higgs VEV)  = 246 GeV
M_Planck          = 1.22 × 10^19 GeV
Ratio             = M_EW/M_Planck ~ 10^-17
```

**The Problem**:
In quantum field theory, the Higgs mass receives quadratic corrections:
```
δm²_H ~ (y²_t/16π²) × Λ²

where Λ = UV cutoff (naively M_Planck)
```

If Λ = M_Planck:
- δm_H ~ 10^17 GeV
- Observed m_H = 125 GeV
- Need cancellation to 1 part in 10^34!

**The Analogy**: Like balancing a pencil on its tip for 14 billion years — technically possible, but suspiciously unlikely without a mechanism.

### Part 2: Supersymmetric Solution

**Mechanism**:
```
Top loop:   δm²_H ~ +y²_t Λ²/(16π²)
Stop loop:  δm²_H ~ -y²_t Λ²/(16π²)  [opposite sign!]
Sum:        δm²_H ~ (m²_stop - m²_top) × log(Λ)
```

The quadratic divergence cancels; only logarithmic remains!

**Requirements**:
- Superpartners not too heavy: m_SUSY < few TeV
- Otherwise fine-tuning reappears

**Current Status**:
| Particle | LHC Bound |
|----------|-----------|
| Gluino | > 2.2 TeV |
| Squark | > 1.5 TeV |
| Stop | > 1.0 TeV |

Fine-tuning measure: Δ ~ (m_stop/m_H)² ~ 100-300

**Verdict**: SUSY viable but pushed into "unnatural" territory by LHC.
