## Part 1: Tidal Dwarf Galaxies

### Physics
- TDGs form from tidal debris of galaxy collisions
- No primordial dark matter halos (ΛCDM prediction)
- MOND: Same BTFR as normal galaxies (a₀ universal)
- Synchronism: Depends on local density profile

### Analysis
Typical TDG:
- M_bar ~ 10^8 M_sun
- R_eff ~ 2 kpc
- Σ ~ 8 M_sun/pc²

This is SIMILAR to normal dwarf galaxies (10-50 M_sun/pc²), so:
- **Synchronism predicts**: Same BTFR as normal galaxies
- **MOND predicts**: Same BTFR as normal galaxies
- **Result**: NOT discriminating

### Observations
- Lelli et al. (2015): NGC 5291 TDGs follow same BTFR
- Flores et al. (2016): VCC 2062 consistent with normal galaxies
- Conclusion: TDGs do not discriminate between theories

## Part 2: Ultra-Diffuse Galaxies

### Physics
UDGs are extreme systems:
- Σ ~ 1-10 M_sun/pc² (vs ~100 for normal disks)
- R_eff > 1.5 kpc
- M_star ~ 10^7 - 10^8 M_sun

### Predictions

**MOND (a₀ universal)**:
- UDGs follow EXACT same BTFR as normal galaxies
- No dependence on surface density

**Synchronism (C depends on ρ)**:
- Low Σ → low ρ → low C → high G_eff
- V_obs/V_bar ~ 1.3× higher than normal galaxies
- **Detectable offset from normal BTFR**

### Quantitative Prediction
For UDG with Σ ~ 1 M_sun/pc² vs normal Σ ~ 50 M_sun/pc²:
```
log(Σ_UDG/Σ_normal) = -1.67
Using slope from Session #87: -0.074
Δlog(V/V_bar) = +0.12
V/V_bar ratio change: 1.33×
```

**This is a DISCRIMINATING TEST!**
