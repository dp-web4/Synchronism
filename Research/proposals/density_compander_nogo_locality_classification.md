## Claim

The "density-compander no-go" (a local function of density `ρ(r)` cannot reproduce the galactic RAR) should **not** be propagated as a novel theorem. It is:

1. A **worked instance of an established constraint** — Milgrom's result that MOND phenomenology requires *strong non-locality* (MOND-as-modified-inertia, astro-ph/0510117), and the empirical fact that the mass discrepancy is **acceleration-keyed** (RAR/MDAR, Lelli–McGaugh–Schombert 2016; Lelli et al. 2017, scatter ≲0.13 dex).
2. Subject to a sharper scope than previously stated: the discriminating axis is **locality of the modification's state variable**, not "density-based." 

## Two corrections to prior framing

- **"Any density-based MOND mimic fails" is wrong.** Verlinde (enclosed `M_B(<r)`), MOG (enclosed mass), and MOND's own surface-density relations (column-integrated `Σ`, Lelli+2016; Donato+2009) are "density-based" colloquially but key on **non-local** quantities and **escape** the no-go. Replace the phrase everywhere with "any mimic keyed on the **local volumetric density `ρ(r)`**."
- **The obstruction is not novel.** Cite Milgrom (2005) as the parent result. Synchronism's `C(ρ)` is the rare ansatz keyed to local `ρ`, which is *why* it is caught — a quantified instance (ΔBIC=+184; 1.7-dex cross-system `ρ↔g_bar` offset; within-cluster flat-ρ), not a new physics theorem.
