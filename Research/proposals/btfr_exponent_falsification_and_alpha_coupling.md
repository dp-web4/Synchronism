## 1. BTFR Exponent n ≈ 2.2 — Likely Already Failed

### The Claim

The site's TEST-09 predicts:  
> "BTFR exponent n ≈ 2.2 universal across bands"  
> Kill criterion: exponent varies by >0.3 across bands

### The Problem

This kill criterion is malformed. The kill criterion tests *band universality* (does n vary across photometric bands?), but the actual observational value of n is fundamentally different from the prediction:

- **Lelli, McGaugh & Schombert (2019, ApJ 886:77)**: n = 3.85 ± 0.09 across 153 galaxies, multi-band
- **McGaugh & Schombert (2015)**: n = 3.95 ± 0.11
- **MOND theoretical prediction**: n = 4 exactly
- **Synchronism prediction**: n ≈ 2.2

The discrepancy is Δn = 1.65 — more than 5× the kill criterion threshold of 0.3. The observed exponent is ~75% larger than predicted.

### Diagnosis

Two possibilities:

1. **The prediction is stated incorrectly.** Perhaps the framework predicts a *residual sub-exponent* or a *specific sub-sample exponent* (e.g., low-surface-brightness galaxies only), and the kill criterion was supposed to test that specific prediction. If so, the prediction needs to be restated with precision.

2. **The prediction is genuinely failed.** If n ≈ 2.2 is the full-sample BTFR exponent, it has been falsified by Lelli et al. 2019 and should be moved to the honest-assessment failure catalog alongside YBCO T_c and the Bullet Cluster viscosity sign error.

### Research Questions

1. What derivation chain in Synchronism produces n ≈ 2.2? Is it a direct consequence of C(ρ)'s functional form, or a post-hoc fit?
2. Is n ≈ 2.2 the predicted exponent for a specific sub-population (e.g., intermediate-mass spirals where the coherence transition overlaps with baryon dynamics), not the full sample?
3. If the full-sample BTFR exponent is falsified, does this constrain the C(ρ) functional form (since the BTFR exponent depends on the deep-MOND asymptotics of the coherence function)?

### Action

- If no derivation: mark TEST-09 as Failed on the site and move to honest-assessment
- If there is a derivation for a specific sub-population: restate the prediction precisely and explain what "n ≈ 2.2" means in that context

## 2. α² in Galactic Coupling — Dimensional Coincidence or Novel Physics?

### The Claim

The site's parameter-derivations page lists:
> A = 4π/(α²GR₀²) ≈ 0.029  
> where α = fine-structure constant ≈ 1/137

This gives ρ_crit = A × V_flat² — the critical density for galaxy rotation.

### Why This Is Alarming

The **fine-structure constant** α is the coupling constant for electromagnetism (quantum electrodynamics). **G** is Newton's gravitational constant. Putting α² into a galactic dynamical formula is extraordinary:

- No version of GR, EFT of gravity, MOND relativizations (TeVeS, AeST), emergent gravity (Verlinde, Jacobson), or any UV completion of gravity (LQG, string theory, asymptotic safety) produces α in a galactic coupling.
- α ≈ 1/137 is dimensionless and appears in the formula purely as a number. Whether α² in this context is "the fine-structure constant" or just "a dimensionless number that happens to equal ≈ (1/137)²" is the critical question.
- At α = 1/137: α² ≈ 5.3 × 10⁻⁵. The formula 4π/(α²GR₀²) — with G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ and R₀ from observed galaxy scales — produces a number that matches observations at the 5% level.

### Three Interpretations

1. **Coincidence**: α² ≈ (1/137)² = a small dimensionless number that happens to set the right scale when combined with G and R₀. This is a "dimensional accident" — a post-hoc numerical fit. The 5% agreement is not evidence of a mechanism; it's evidence that the combination of constants can be tuned to produce the right order of magnitude.

2. **Electromagnetic-gravitational unification**: If α² genuinely appears in galactic dynamics, it would imply a deep connection between electromagnetism and gravity at cosmological scales — something no current theory predicts. This would be a standalone publishable discovery requiring a Lagrangian.

3. **Reparametrization of a known result**: Perhaps A = 0.029 can be derived from a combination of known constants that has nothing to do with α, and the α² form is a coincidental factorization. The coincidence A ≈ 4π/(α²GR₀²) would then be numerology, not physics.

### Research Questions

1. What is the original derivation of A = 0.029? Which research session introduced α² into this formula?
2. Can A be expressed without α, using purely gravitational/cosmological constants? If A = f(c, G, H₀, ...) with no α, then the α² form is numerology.
3. If α² is fundamental: write the Lagrangian. A galactic coupling that involves the electromagnetic fine-structure constant is a paper, not a parameter.
4. If α² is coincidental: relabel A as a dimensional fit constant. 5% agreement is not derivation.

### Action

- Search research archive for the A = 4π/(α²GR₀²) derivation (Sessions #66, #87-91)
- Determine whether α appears in the derivation or is a post-hoc factorization
- If post-hoc: relabel the parameter as "dimensional fit" and update the site badge from "Validated" to "Speculative | Dimensional Fit"
- If genuine: write a standalone derivation document and flag it as the framework's most surprising result
