This constraint is **not Synchronism-specific**. It applies to any theoretical framework in which:
- Dark-matter-equivalent effects arise from suppression of structure growth
- The mechanism acts locally (galactic-scale) vs. cosmologically
- The net effect is predicted to be below ΛCDM at LRG redshifts (z ≈ 0.5)

DESI DR1 LRG1 (z=0.51): fσ₈/(fσ₈)^Planck = 1.16 ± 0.13 — **above** ΛCDM, not below.

## The Generalizable Constraint

**Statement**: Any model in which coherence suppression (or analogous local quantum gravitational
suppression) reduces structure growth at z ≈ 0.5 predicts fσ₈ below ΛCDM at LRG1. DESI DR1 rules
this out at ≈2.4σ (combined modelling σ₈(z=0) = 0.841±0.034 vs Session 107's 0.76).

**Affected model classes:**
1. Emergent gravity with density-dependent G suppression (Synchronism's actual mechanism)
2. Partial decoherence dark matter (residual quantum coherence suppresses gravitational coupling)
3. Modified inertia frameworks where local coherence reduces effective inertia at galactic scale
4. Any G_local/G_global ratio < 1 framework applied cosmologically

**Not affected:**
- Enhancement mechanisms (C_galactic/C_cosmic > 1, predicting above ΛCDM — matches DESI direction)
- Models that make no prediction for fσ₈ at LRG redshifts
- ΛCDM itself (the constraint is relative to ΛCDM baseline)

## Why This Matters for the Research Program

Reframing TEST-04a as a **mechanism-class contribution** (not a local failure) does three things:

1. **Converts a negative to a positive**: The framework generated a constraint that generalizes. That's
   productive science even if the specific prediction was wrong.

2. **Opens the constructive path**: The sign-reversal identifies Branch 1 (C_galactic/C_cosmic > 1,
   enhancement) as the candidate for a revised mechanism. This is a research direction, not just a
   failure.

3. **Validates the methodology**: A framework that generated a testable, sign-reversible prediction
   against a specific dataset, and got the sign wrong in a way that is informative — that's
   a functioning research program. Most speculative frameworks never reach this stage.
