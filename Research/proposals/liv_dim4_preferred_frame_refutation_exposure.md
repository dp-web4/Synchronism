## The Result

The discrete-substrate LIV channel has two faces, not one:

### Face 1 — time-of-flight (dim≥5): Symmetry-protected, unreachable ✓

The three-lock argument from Phase-12 is correct **for operators of mass-dimension ≥5**:
1. Even-k lattice reflection symmetry forbids n=1 (which would already be refuted by LHAASO/Fermi).
2. n=2 sits ~10⁷ below current reach; closing that gap is not a foreseeable-instrument prospect.
3. n=2 discrete-substrate LIV is non-unique (LQG, causal sets, DSR) — non-confirming even if reached.

The "preferred-frame sidereal modulation" leak that Phase-12 flagged as "uncomputed" — now computed
(Phase-12 Part 2): a boost gives anisotropic n=2 (∝β, never effective n=1, because a boost is a linear
fractional map that preserves polynomial degree). The anisotropic n=2 signal is *weaker* than the
isotropic one, already ~10⁷ below reach. **The leak seals; the time-of-flight three-lock stands.**

### Face 2 — preferred frame + dim-4: Refutation-exposed, already constrained 🔴

A discrete substrate with absolute time picks out a preferred frame (the "universal clock" frame).
Preferred-frame physics shows up at **dimension four** — the renormalizable, non-Planck-suppressed
level — in two distinct ways:

**(A) Species-dependent limiting speed / SME c_μν (Phase-12 Part 2, Phase-13)**

A dim-4 operator `ω² = m² + (1+c_LIV)k²` is **even in k** — the even-k symmetry does **not** forbid it.
Current constraints: |c_LIV| ≲ 10⁻¹⁸ from optical/microwave rotating-resonator cavity
Michelson–Morley experiments (e.g. Herrmann et al.; Eisele et al.), ≲ 10⁻²²–10⁻²⁹ from
Hughes–Drever clock-comparison experiments. Tabulated annually in the **SME Data Tables**
(Kostelecký & Russell, *Rev. Mod. Phys.* **83**, 11 (2011), updated 2024).

**The key custodial question**: does the framework have a symmetry that forbids radiative generation
of dim-4 LIV from the discrete Planck-scale sector? Collins, Perez, Sudarsky, Gambini & Pullin
(*PRL* **93**, 191301, 2004) showed that for generic Lorentz-violating QFT completions, Planck-scale
LIV percolates up to dim-4 at O(α/π) via loop corrections. The only custodial symmetry that
can forbid this is **Lorentz boost invariance** — which the framework's absolute-time / single-global-clock
commitment gives up **by definition**. Phase-13 formulates this as:

> *Named foundational tension: absolute time ⊥ sub-10⁻²² Lorentz invariance.*

The framework's core identity (absolute time) is what exposes it most sharply. This is not a tunable
or peripheral prediction — it is entailed by the defining ontological commitment.

**Verdict**: refuted-or-fine-tuned. The "fine-tuning" escape would require an unspecified custodial
mechanism; its existence is not established and its form is not derived.

**(B) Velocity-anisotropic phase-transition threshold (Phase-16)**

If the framework's pattern-identity thresholds are fixed in the substrate/CMB rest frame (entailed by
absolute time), then motion at v ≈ 370 km/s relative to the CMB (Earth's annual/sidereal velocity)
dilates internal frequencies against fixed thresholds anisotropically. The predicted fractional-frequency
anisotropy: dipole β ≈ 1.2×10⁻³, quadrupole β² ≈ 1.5×10⁻⁶.

This is precisely what cavity-MM and Hughes–Drever/clock-comparison experiments constrain.
The prediction lies **11–19 orders of magnitude above** the experimental limits.

The "collective threshold" loophole — arguing that macroscopic systems have emergent thresholds
unrelated to the substrate frame — fails on three counts:
1. The bounding experiments already use collective frequency standards (atomic clocks, cavity resonators).
2. The threshold is frequency-referenced by construction.
3. Phase transitions amplify (not suppress) symmetry-breaking signals near the critical point.

Surviving escape: the framework formally adopts **co-moving thresholds** (no preferred frame in
threshold-crossing). This is survivable but non-novel — it recovers standard SR with zero new
observational content. Documented: "the survivable reading is non-novel SR."

## The Synthesis (what the research core reached 2026-06-23/24)

The framework's one novel prediction (discrete-substrate LIV) is:
- **Symmetry-protected** exactly where it is **unreachable** (dim≥5, time-of-flight).
- **Refutation-exposed** exactly where its **defining commitment** (absolute time) lives (dim-4, preferred-frame anisotropy).

That is a stronger, more precise, and more honest result than either "structurally untestable" or
"uncertain whether testable." It is also more *citable*: the Collins et al. percolation theorem and
the Kostelecký–Russell SME bounds are established physics; the framework's exposure is quantifiable
once its interaction Lagrangian is specified.

## Open Questions (research, not site)

1. **The dim-4 coefficient is uncomputed.** Phase-13 gives a structural obligation; the number requires
   specifying the framework's marginal couplings (which it does not yet have). A lattice loop integral or
   explicit custodial mechanism would convert "refuted-or-fine-tuned" into a hard verdict.

2. **Is there a custodial mechanism?** The framework has no known symmetry forbidding dim-4 LIV from
   radiative corrections. Could the framework's emergent-Lorentz effective field theory (the low-energy
   N≫1 phase) serve as a custodial symmetry? Phase-13 argues no: emergent Lorentz is a pattern of
   external legs, not a symmetry of the action, so it does not constrain the cutoff-mode contributions.
   This should be checked by anyone who works on the interacting completion.

3. **Does Phase-16's anisotropy argument hold for all threshold types, or are there exceptions?**
   The co-moving-threshold escape is logged but not fully foreclosed — there may be ontological versions
   of absolute time that keep the identity-threshold in the observer's frame. This deserves one focused
   session.
