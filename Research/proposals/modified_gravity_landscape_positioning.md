## The Comparison

These frameworks all enter the same phenomenological territory as C(ρ):

| Framework | How it gets a₀ | Structure |
|-----------|----------------|-----------|
| MOND (Milgrom 1983) | Empirical interpolation | μ-function, no dynamics |
| Verlinde emergent gravity (2016) | Entropy gradient in dark energy volume | Field-theoretic, Newtonian limit gives MOND |
| TeVeS (Bekenstein 2004) | Tensor-vector-scalar field + non-standard kinetic term | Field theory, Lorentz-covariant |
| MOG / STVG (Moffat 2006) | Massive vector field with running coupling | Field theory, post-Newtonian predictions |
| Conformal gravity (Mannheim) | Conformal Weyl tensor action | Field theory, rotation curves from different mechanism |
| Synchronism C(ρ) | Compander function ρ → C, calibrated A·V_flat² | Static evaluative map, no dynamics |

## The Key Question

**Is C(ρ) a sub-case, extension, or reparametrization of Verlinde's entropic gravity?**

Verlinde's framework:
- Derives a₀ from the entropy budget of the Hubble volume
- Predicts MOND-like rotation curves as an emergent thermodynamic effect
- Makes predictions in regimes beyond MOND (e.g., clusters, galaxy-scale dark-matter apparent distribution)
- Was tested by Brouwer et al. (2017) against KiDS gravitational lensing and found consistent at the ~1σ level

C(ρ) differs in structure: it maps local density to a coherence parameter, not a gravitational modification. But in the galaxy-rotation regime, free-γ → MOND identically (SPARC RAR, 2026-05-21). So:

1. If C(ρ) is calibrated to produce MOND-like galaxy dynamics, and Verlinde produces MOND-like galaxy dynamics from entropic gravity, are the two frameworks related or independent?

2. Verlinde's framework makes cluster-scale predictions (apparent dark matter as elasticity of spacetime) that TeVeS fails on (Bullet Cluster bullet-side requires dark matter in TeVeS). Does C(ρ) make any cluster-scale prediction beyond the Bullet Cluster sign error already documented?

3. The compander C(ρ) has no field-theoretic completion — it's an evaluative map, not a field equation. Verlinde has a (contested) field theory. Does this structural difference imply different empirical predictions anywhere?

## Research Directions

**Path A — Classify the relationship**: Can C(ρ) galaxy dynamics be derived from Verlinde's framework as a special case? If yes, C(ρ) is a reparametrization of Verlinde in the galaxy regime.

**Path B — Find the divergence**: Is there any regime (cluster scale, high-redshift, extreme-low-acceleration) where C(ρ) and Verlinde make quantitatively different predictions? If yes, this is the only remaining discriminating test.

**Path C — Accept MOND-class**: If C(ρ) collapses onto MOND (already shown by SPARC, 2026-05-21), and MOND collapses onto Verlinde in the low-acceleration limit (Verlinde 2016), then C(ρ) is a special-case reparametrization of Verlinde in the galaxy regime. State this explicitly.

## Why This Matters for the Site

The site is missing the landscape comparison that P4-level readers want. Honest Assessment has a "What's Untested" section — it should also have "Related frameworks and how C(ρ) compares to them." This is not a disclosure of failure; it's a classification of where C(ρ) sits in the landscape.

Without this, a modified-gravity reader closes the tab at the MOND attribution without seeing whether Synchronism has a structural difference from Verlinde.
