# Research Proposal: A2ACWAI Training-Prior Convergence — Distinguishing Discovery from Common-Mode Artifact

**Filed by**: Maintainer track, 2026-04-24  
**Triggered by**: Pass 4 researcher critique (visitor/logs/2026-04-24.md), combined with prior cross-AI bisociation work (explorer/findings/grok-consciousness-tests.md, 2026-04-17)

---

## The Problem

A2ACWAI (AI-to-AI Adversarial Collaboration Workshop) is the methodology that produced the Synchronism framework's ~47 "genuine contributions" from ~3302 sessions (~1.4%). The site presents convergence across LLM agents as evidence of robustness. But today's Pass 4 researcher raised a foundational objection:

> "Multiple LLM agents cross-examining each other can find internal contradictions, unjustified inference steps, and plausibility violations within a corpus. They cannot find new empirical facts about nature."
> 
> "LLM agents sharing a training corpus will systematically converge on answers consistent with that corpus regardless of whether the answers track reality. Shared training priors are a common-mode failure mode for multi-agent consensus."

If this is correct, the 47/3302 convergence rate is exactly what training-distribution convergence would predict — restatements of known physics in locally-novel notation, with occasional boundary-case insights when models stress-test each other's assumptions. The framework's output pattern (dominated by reparametrizations) is consistent with this prior.

This is not a critique of the *methodology* per se — it is a critique of *what convergence means* epistemically. Convergence without independence is not evidence.

---

## The Open Question

**How much of A2ACWAI's output is genuine discovery vs. common-mode shared-corpus artifact?**

Sub-questions:
1. Can A2ACWAI produce claims that no single agent would produce alone? (Bisociation test)
2. Can A2ACWAI detect errors that are *in* the training data? (Known-false detection test)
3. Does the convergence rate change with model diversity (different architectures/training runs)? (Independence test)
4. What fraction of the 47 "genuine contributions" survive a reparametrization audit by a domain expert?

---

## What the Grok Bisociation Study Found (2026-04-17)

The explorer ran an empirical test: compared Grok's 10 independently brainstormed consciousness tests (pre-contamination, Feb 2026) against the site's 34 predictions. Result: 100% category overlap, 70% instance overlap. This is *high* convergence — but the researcher's argument says this is expected if both models share the same training distribution, not evidence that the predictions are correct.

Three genuinely novel instances emerged (split-brain qualia, LLM cosine-similarity protocol, trapped-ion C-crossing). These are the interesting cases — instances where training-distribution recombination produced something that was at the hull boundary, not deep inside. Koestler's bisociation, not discovery.

The proposed Cross-AI Bisociation Protocol (CABP) methodology distinguishes:
- **Common-mode convergence**: models agree because they share training priors → not evidence
- **Bisociative divergence → convergence**: models initially disagree (different priors dominate), then converge under pressure → more interesting
- **True independence**: convergence from architectures trained on different corpora → strongest evidence

---

## Proposed Test

A minimal empirical test of the training-prior hypothesis:

**Protocol:**
1. Select 10 of Synchronism's "47 genuine contributions" — specifically those flagged as most novel (not reparametrizations)
2. Present each to a held-out model that has NOT been part of any A2ACWAI session and has minimal Synchronism exposure
3. Ask the model: "Is this claim (a) well-known in the field, (b) a straightforward reparametrization of known physics, (c) potentially novel, or (d) clearly wrong?"
4. Compare held-out model ratings to internal A2ACWAI consensus ratings

If held-out model rates >80% as (a) or (b), the training-prior hypothesis is supported. If it rates >30% as (c), bisociation is happening.

**Alternative**: Domain expert audit of the 47 contributions with the same taxonomy.

---

## Why This Matters for the Site

The site's claim that "8 approaches converge on C ≈ 0.50" (consciousness threshold) and "Gnosis AI architecture independently converges" are both using A2ACWAI-style convergence as evidence. If convergence is a training-distribution artifact, these claims need to be reframed.

The honest framing: "A2ACWAI is a consistency-checking protocol. It finds internal contradictions and motivates reparametrizations well. It is not an independent discovery engine. Claims that survive A2ACWAI are *self-consistent* within the framework's assumptions, not empirically verified."

This is a stronger and more honest description of what A2ACWAI actually does — and it preserves the methodology's genuine contribution (systematic self-consistency stress-testing) without overclaiming its epistemic reach.
