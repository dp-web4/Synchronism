## Three Resolution Options

### Option A: Name the Distinguishing Experiment

If one measurement exists that would return different results under Synchronism vs standard physics + MOND + decoherence, name it explicitly and front-load it.

- Candidate: TEST-02 wide-binary density dependence (if the wide-binary anomaly resolves in favor of Chae, and if ξ(ρ) can be derived)
- Candidate: Partition function route — derive C(ρ) as the fixed point of a self-consistency equation, which would make it a dynamical law not just a functional choice
- Candidate: A compander-family AIC/BIC comparison that shows *why* tanh is privileged over Hill/logistic/erf

If none of these yield a distinguishing prediction, Option A is not currently available.

### Option B: Explicit Interpretation + Methodology Classification

Classify Synchronism as:
1. **An interpretation** of quantum mechanics and astrophysics — like Bohmian mechanics or relational QM, evaluated by parsimony, elegance, and explanatory economy, not by novel empirical predictions
2. **A methodology contribution** — A2ACW protocol, validation badges, kill criteria, pre-registration discipline, mechanism-class failure taxonomy

This is honest, defensible, and actually describes what the site has built. The honest-assessment page is already the most rigorous self-audit infrastructure of any physics-adjacent site reviewed. Lead with that.

The site's meta-contribution — **running 3,308 AI-to-AI adversarial physics sessions, documenting a 0% novel-survivor rate, and building a reproducible self-audit infrastructure** — is publishable as a methodology paper. The physics is the substrate it was developed on.

### Option C: Define a New Classification

"Productive scaffolding" — the 2026-04-09 explorer finding identified that wrong equations can generate right questions via structural scaffolding (Bohr-Sommerfeld pattern). Synchronism is neither interpretation nor theory; it is a **heuristic generator** — a wrong model that asks the right questions.

This would be a new classification in the philosophy of science literature. It is more ambitious than Option B but requires a worked-out argument.

## Recommendation

**Option B now, Option A if/when TEST-02 executes.**

Explicitly classify the framework as:
- An **interpretation** of known physics in coherence language (ontological reframe, not new physics)
- A **methodology research program** (A2ACW, self-audit infrastructure, mechanism-class failure taxonomy)

The site should say this on the landing page, on `/what-synchronism-is-not`, and on the honest assessment. The current middle position — "ontological reframe that might have novel predictions" — is the hardest to defend and the least compelling pitch.

The methodology contribution is publishable without the physics being confirmed. The interpretation claim is defensible without being testable. Both are honest. Neither requires pretending there are novel confirmed predictions.
