## The Three Transferable Nulls

The Researcher identifies three results that survive the framework's refutation and are **independently publishable**:

### 1. Local-Density No-Go / Locality Triage
Any gravity modification keyed on a local intensive density variable ρ(r) cannot reproduce an acceleration-keyed (non-local) RAR across systems with different mass geometries except by per-system calibration.

- **Quantified:** ΔBIC=+184 (SPARC, γ=2); ~1.7 dex cross-system ρ↔g_bar offset; ρ_crit,cluster off by 10⁴–10⁶.
- **Lineage:** Milgrom's non-locality theorem (astro-ph/0510117) — this is a quantified *instance*, not a novel theorem.
- **Contribution:** The locality triage (which proposals escape: enclosed-mass, acceleration, surface-density) is a useful sorting of the contemporary modgrav model space.
- **Publication candidate:** "Locality bounds on density-compander dark-sector alternatives to MOND" — framed as a constraint on a *class* of models, citing Milgrom up top.

### 2. Mechanism-Class DESI Constraint
DESI DR1 full-shape disfavors *any* framework predicting uniform, scale-independent late-time suppression of structure growth — at ~2.15σ (LRG1, z=0.51), triggering the pre-registered kill criterion.

- **Quantified:** LRG1 fσ8/fid = 1.16±0.13 ABOVE ΛCDM; Synchronism-form suppression (factor 0.88) disfavored 2.15σ.
- **Mechanism-class reach:** This is the site's only transferable physics result — it generalizes to all growth-suppression dark-matter frameworks, not just Synchronism.
- **Publication candidate:** "DESI DR1 growth disfavors uniform scale-independent late-time growth suppression: a mechanism-class constraint."
- **Caveat:** Single-bin result at ~2σ; pre-registered criterion fires but below nominal kill threshold. DR2 unfreeze condition: fσ8(z≈0.5) ≤ 0.46.

### 3. A2ACW Program-Level Null
Same-corpus adversarial AI pairs (AI-to-AI Adversarial Collaboration Workshop) can detect internal consistency failures but cannot generate or detect out-of-distribution novelty — specificity 0/6 against held-out genuine discoveries (COBE, Higgs, GW detection all false-flagged).

- **Quantified:** Sensitivity high (catches reparametrizations); specificity 0/6 (genuine OOD discoveries pass same-corpus self-play unchallenged).
- **Diagnosis:** The boundary is the oracle, not the ambition. Systems with a non-corpus external oracle (FunSearch, AlphaEvolve, GNoME) produce genuine novelty; same-corpus self-play converges on internal consistency, not discovery.
- **Publication candidate:** "Adversarial AI self-play over shared training corpora: a reparametrization detector, not a discovery engine." Directly relevant to the "AI scientist" discourse.

## The Strategy Question

**Should the program shift from daily auditing → external preprint packaging?**

Arguments FOR shifting:
1. The site has reached a genuine fixed point. The daily loop is now finding polish issues (symbol hygiene, badge consistency, onboarding flow) rather than substantive errors or new falsifications. This is a completion signal, not a failure signal.
2. The three transferable nulls are the real outputs. They are more legible as standalone preprints than as items in a framework's failure log. A researcher browsing the site for the density-compander constraint gets a better product from an arXiv preprint than from `/honest-assessment`.
3. External publication of the nulls creates a feedback loop from the broader community — reviewers may identify errors or extensions the self-contained loop cannot.

Arguments AGAINST (or for continued daily loop):
1. The daily loop is cheap relative to preprint preparation. A 4-persona browse costs a few hours of compute and surfaces genuine friction. Preprint preparation costs months of human time (writing, revision, response to reviewers).
2. The site serves a different audience than arXiv. The "site reaches a fixed point" diagnosis applies to the *physics* content, not the *accessibility* work. Onboarding improvements (equation reveal order, badge tooltips, expert-note visibility) still improve the experience for a broad audience.
3. The A2ACW preprint is the most AI-community-relevant result but requires the CCH metric to be validated externally (currently admitted as nominal).

**Proposed resolution:** The two activities are not mutually exclusive. The daily loop can continue at reduced cadence or reduced scope (accessibility/polish focus) while the preprint effort runs on a separate track. The loop's current value is in the accessibility layer; the physics layer has converged.
