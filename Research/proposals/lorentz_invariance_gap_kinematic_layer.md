## Research Question

Two possible resolutions:

**Option A — IR limit derivation**: Show that the discrete-lattice substrate with parallel update
recovers an effective Lorentz-invariant continuum in some infrared limit. This would require
specifying:
1. A lattice geometry (cubic? hexagonal? random?)
2. An update rule that is locally isotropic in the IR
3. A derivation that SO(3) emerges as an approximate symmetry at scales >> lattice spacing

Compare: staggered fermion lattice QCD recovers Lorentz invariance in the continuum limit with
specific staggering structure. A random lattice (Regge calculus) can also recover this. Neither
is trivial.

**Option B — Scope restriction**: Explicitly restrict the framework to non-relativistic domains
where the discrete substrate is a valid description (condensed matter, galactic dynamics at
sub-relativistic velocities, neural dynamics). This would:
- Make all MOND and dark-matter predictions more honest (effective non-relativistic theory)
- Cleanly scope away cosmological claims (BAO, CMB, inflation) that require relativistic treatment
- Allow the quantum predictions to be stated as non-relativistic approximations with known
  relativistic corrections TBD

is non-relativistic without a shown IR limit. The gap is the framework's strongest structural
honesty moment — acknowledging it publicly, as an open research question with the two candidate
paths, would be more credible than the current framing.

tried" is more interesting to a researcher than one that buries the gap in an honest-assessment
footnote while leading with universality.
