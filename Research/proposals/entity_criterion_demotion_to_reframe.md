## The Case for Demotion

The condition Γ ≪ m is the standard **narrow-resonance / narrow-width condition** from quantum field theory:

- It is the requirement for a Breit-Wigner resonance to be a well-defined isolated pole in the Källén-Lehmann spectral representation.
- Every QFT student learns that states with Γ/m ≫ 1 (e.g., f₀(500), σ/κ, ρ meson at broad extraction) are problematic as particle states, while narrow states (J/ψ, Υ, etc.) are well-defined quasiparticles.
- The PDG itself implicitly applies this condition when flagging broad states as "needs confirmation" or excluding them from summary tables.

The site's entity criterion page body text already conceded: "Standard QFT treats Γ ≪ m as a practical approximation (narrow resonances are easier to compute), not a fundamental threshold for 'entity-hood'." The page then frames Synchronism's contribution as "an ontological interpretation, not the condition itself." This is the correct framing — and it makes the condition a Reparametrization, not a novel prediction.

## What Synchronism Adds (and Does Not Add)

**What it adds**: An ontological interpretation — the condition Γ < m is claimed to mark "coherence cycle completion" in the oscillation basis. This is a physical picture (narrative), not a new mathematical result.

**What it does not add**: A new condition, new predictions, new observables. The proposed PDG audit would classify resonances by Γ/m — exactly what the PDG already does informally. Any discriminating result would need to show that the Synchronism classification predicts something QFT's resonance taxonomy does not already predict, with independent evidence. This has not been demonstrated.
