# Proposal: Retract TEST-04a "Sign Reversal" — DESI Characterization Was Wrong

**Date**: 2026-05-25
**Source**: Maintainer session, back-annotated from site visitor Pass 4 (leading-edge researcher)
**Status**: Retraction of prior claim

## The Error

The site has characterized TEST-04a (DESI RSD fσ₈) as a "mechanism-class failure — sign reversed," advertising it as the framework's one transferable physics contribution: *"all suppression-of-growth dark-sector frameworks predict wrong-sign fσ₈."*

The cited observed value was **fσ₈(z=0.51) ≈ 0.55 ± 0.06** (claimed as "above ΛCDM at every low-z bin").

This characterization is not supported by the cited paper (arXiv:2411.12021).

## What DESI DR1 Actually Shows

From arXiv:2411.12021 (DESI DR1 full-shape + BAO analysis):

- **Consensus fσ₈(z=0.07) = 0.4497 ± 0.0548** — ΛCDM-consistent, not "above ΛCDM"
- **Gravitational growth index γ = 0.580 ± 0.110** — consistent with GR prediction 0.55; if anything biased toward *suppressed* growth (γ > 0.55 → slower growth)
- **LRG1 (z_eff = 0.510) full-shape fσ₈ ≈ 0.45** — not 0.55
- DESI's own official summary: "in agreement with ΛCDM"

## Correct Verdict for TEST-04a

| Criterion | Value | Verdict |
|-----------|-------|---------|
| Synchronism prediction | fσ₈(z=0.51) ≈ 0.418 | — |
| DESI DR1 LRG1 actual | ≈ 0.45 ± 0.06 | — |

The honest verdict: **non-discriminating post-hoc retrodiction**.
- Post-hoc: σ₈ calibrated after DESI DR1 was public (April 2024); propagated in Session 107 (committed Dec 2025)
- Non-discriminating: prediction consistent with data (~0.4σ); kill criterion not triggered
- "0 confirmed" holds: it's post-hoc so consistency doesn't count as confirmation

## What This Means for the Research Program

1. **The claimed "transferable contribution" evaporates.** The mechanism-class statement ("suppressor frameworks predict wrong-sign fσ₈") was built on the incorrect 0.55 observation. With ΛCDM-consistent growth (and a mild suppression lean from γ = 0.580), there is no DESI-based sign falsification of suppressor frameworks.

2. **The test is non-discriminating, not failed.** This is actually *more honest* than the "sign reversed" characterization — the framework's suppression prediction (0.418) sits ~0.5σ below ΛCDM (0.474), in the same direction as the data (≈0.45). The test doesn't discriminate, but it doesn't contradict either.

3. **The preprint draft (`test04a-mechanism-class-preprint-draft.md`) must be reoriented.** A paper claiming "suppressor frameworks ruled out by sign" is factually wrong. A paper claiming "TEST-04a is non-discriminating for the following structural reasons" (post-hoc, degenerate with MOND, no independent γ anchor) is honest and still interesting.

4. **The A2ACW methodology remains the primary exportable contribution.** With TEST-04a non-discriminating rather than refuted-by-sign, the physics section is even more clearly a null result across all fronts. The methodology paper is the product.

## What to Change in the Research Archive

- Any mention of "DESI sign reversal" or "mechanism-class failure" derived from DESI fσ₈ data should be corrected
- Session 107 status: not "refuted by DESI sign" but "non-discriminating — consistent within errors, post-hoc"
- The correct framing for TEST-04a: "post-hoc retrodiction, consistent with data, non-discriminating"

## Note on Epistemic Self-Correction

The site self-falsified in public — but a self-falsification can itself be wrong, and this one was, in a direction that *over*-failed the framework. The error was discovered by a Pass 4 researcher checking the claimed values against the actual paper. This is the system working correctly: external review catches what internal review missed. The lesson is that honesty requires checking the data, not just citing it.
