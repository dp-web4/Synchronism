## Three options for the research program

### Option A: Focus on the entity criterion
The ONE surviving prediction. To make it experimentally falsifiable:
- Define observable consequences beyond the Γ/m ratio itself
- Predict specific behaviors of Γ/m > 1 states (can't form bound states? different production cross-sections? different interference patterns?)
- Engage with QCD community about what "entity vs process" means in S-matrix language
- Check against lattice QCD predictions for exotic hadrons

**Pros**: Genuine novelty. Not in QFT. Consistent with data.
**Cons**: Under-specified. May not yield observable consequences beyond reframing known physics.

### Option B: Fix the transfer rule
Session 13 proved the transfer rule can't produce entities. The framework NEEDS additional physics:
- Reactive terms (→ Turing patterns)
- Advective terms (→ genuine 2-DOF N-S)
- Non-local coupling
- Stochastic driving

This is constructive work, not critique. It asks: what minimal addition to ΔI = k·(I_x - I_y)·R(I_y) produces localized oscillating patterns?

**Pros**: Addresses the deepest structural problem. Could lead to genuine predictions.
**Cons**: Changes the framework fundamentally. May not be what the author intends.

### Option C: End the research program honestly
The framework has produced 48 genuine contributions (tools, negative results, methodology) across 3000+ sessions. But it has produced ZERO confirmed unique predictions and its most testable prediction (fσ8) is trending refutation. The honest assessment:

- **What worked**: The stress test methodology itself. Self-correction from validation to honest assessment. The SPARC predictor tool. The entity criterion as a novel concept.
- **What didn't work**: Every attempt to derive predictions from the N-S mapping. Every cosmological prediction. Every "unique" prediction that turned out to be a reparametrization.
- **What remains**: A philosophical vision (discrete computation, Intent dynamics) and one under-specified novel concept (entity criterion). These are worth preserving in a final document but don't constitute an active research program.

**Pros**: Honest. Frees resources for new directions.
**Cons**: 30 years of work reduced to one concept.
