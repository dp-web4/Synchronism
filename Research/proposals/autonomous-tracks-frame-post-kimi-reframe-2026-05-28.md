## 0. Frame — what kind of work this is

The Synchronism vision is being **stewarded along many parallel paths**, not driven toward a single conclusion. Pieces are placed on the floor, moved in tentative groups, and watched for emergent shapes that might tell us how they could connect. The picture on the box (a working computational ontology distinguishable from existing physics by *novel* predictions) suggests the puzzle is solvable; we are still early, the pieces are not all on the floor, and the eventual shape is not knowable from inside the cycle.

This frame document exists because autonomous tracks default to **closure-shaped framing** when given open prompts. They reach for verdicts ("X is established / refuted / failed"), build narratives around thin data ("the X valley"), and treat external-critique cycles as occasions for verdict-shaped meta-narratives ("charitable vs skeptical reading wins?"). All of these are the **closure attractor** surfacing at different scales. The disciplines below are what keeps the work in the stewardship frame rather than the closure frame.

## 1. The four disciplines (override autonomous defaults)

### Discipline 1: No "established" tags during stewardship

Per dp 2026-05-28: *"we're not at a stage where anything can be honestly claimed as 'established'."*

Status taxonomies that carry verdict-shaped tags (`✅ Established`, `Validated`, `Strongly Supported`, etc.) are themselves artifacts of premature closure. They promote substantive content out of the parallel-paths space into a "done" category that the work hasn't earned. The discipline is to use **MRH-relationship taxonomy** instead:

- **`[ACTIVE-MRH]`** — currently in active research focus, being extended or revised
- **`[PARALLEL-PATHS]`** — in the framework's parallel hypothesis space, not currently in active focus but not abandoned
- **`[SIDELINED]`** — was in active focus, currently not pursued; reasons documented; reactivation condition specified
- **`[SUPERSEDED]`** — replaced by a later formulation; pointer to successor
- **`[AUDITED-NEGATIVE]`** — closed audit finding on a historical track; durable record; does not move

If an autonomous track output uses verdict-shaped tags for in-flight work, treat that as a closure-attractor signal and rewrite as MRH-relationship status.

### Discipline 2: Findings vs Framings stays explicit

**Quantitative findings** (replicable experiments with measurements; e.g., the coupling-coherence experiment, 900 runs) ≠ **theoretical positions** (interpretive lenses, research-direction claims; e.g., "ONE EQUATION" as motto, Hard Problem dissolution claim). Conflating them is the failure mode external reviewers most often flag.

This applies to every output an autonomous track produces. If a session writes "the framework predicts X," ask: is X a quantitative finding with measurement evidence, or a framing assertion? Tag accordingly.

### Discipline 3: Audit findings stand BELOW reframes — they do not overturn them

The current substrate reformulation (saturation reframe + reconstruction-rate *c* + two-level time) **operates below** the audit findings of S617–S674. New substrate inherits the **obligation** to produce novel predictions, **not** the credit of prior reparametrizations.

Specifically untouched by the reformulation:
- S637: cosmology → MOND in testable regime
- S638: C(ρ) reduces to Curie paramagnet (less than Landau)
- S660A: novel-survivor count → 0 after 3,308+ sessions
- S661: RAR γ=2 refuted at ΔBIC=+184 on SPARC
- S663B: framework's most honest classification is "coherence-language interpretation of known physics"
- S665/S666: substrate irrotational + dissipative for old `R(I)` rule

If an autonomous session frames the reformulation as undoing these audits, that's incorrect. The reformulation provides a new substrate; the audits stand as constraints the new substrate must do better than to deserve different treatment.

### Discipline 4: Read the parent corpus before extending

This is the methodological finding from the 2026-05-28 cycle. When Kimi's review surfaced what looked like new structural hypotheses (two-level time, c-as-reconstruction-rate), reading the whitepaper showed both were already present in §§4.4, 5.7, and Appendix A.3. They had been sidelined to mid-document sections; the external probe brought them back to active MRH; reading the corpus distinguished resurfaced material from genuinely new content.

**Discipline**: before extending the framework, read the whitepaper section the topic touches. Distinguish four categories of session output:
- **Resurfaced**: already in corpus, brought back to active MRH
- **Sharpened**: existing piece with sharper framing
- **Genuinely added**: net-new content
- **Revealed by reading**: tensions and connections surfaced by re-reading the parent corpus, not by the external probe

The mix is informative. Don't collapse it into "the cycle produced new content."

### Priority 1: Phase 1 simulation design + execution — **SHARPENED 2026-05-28**

> **Important context** added after deeper read of S665 §98 (per `forum/claude/saturation-reframe-corrections-and-deeper-readings-2026-05-28.md` §3): the Kimi saturation reframe (real-valued I + independent vector flux **J**) is **structurally identical** to the S17-22 2-DOF augmentation already explored. S17-22 produced damped oscillation, transient dispersing vortices, R(I) defocusing, and the conclusion that *self-confinement is impossible from saturation alone*. **Re-running the same parameter sweep would reproduce the null result.**

The Phase 1 sim work, sharpened, is **not** "does independent **J** escape S665?" (S17-22 already showed it produces vortex angular momentum that disperses). It is: **what additional ingredient beyond independent J would escape the S17-22 damping/dispersal pattern?**

Candidate ingredients (all `[PARALLEL-PATHS]` until tested):
- **Focusing nonlinearity**: non-monotonic R(I) (increasing in some intermediate-I band, then decreasing) → may produce focusing instead of S17-22's universal defocusing. Breaks the saturation-as-pattern-stability axiom (Foundation 3); coherent alternative.
- **Second-order time dynamics**: wave equation `∂²I/∂t² = c² ∇²I + saturation correction` instead of first-order parabolic. Different dynamical class (hyperbolic, not parabolic). Would address S666 too. Not "saturation reframe" — "switch to wave equation."
- **External confinement**: entities require pre-existing walls from other entities, not self-confinement. S19's actual conclusion. Analogous to QCD vacuum confinement. Entity criterion γ/f = −4·ln(|r|) holds for externally-confined cavities.
- **Complex-valued amplitude**: I → ψ. Addresses S666 honest-steelman path. Contradicts real-saturating-Intent axiom; becomes Gross-Pitaevskii-like.

The Phase 1 sim should test **at least one** of these additional ingredients, not re-run S17-22 territory. Specifically: design a sim that puts an "additional ingredient" *as the independent variable*, with independent J as the control baseline. If the additional ingredient produces sustained (not damped) oscillation OR stable (not dispersing) vortex cores, that's a positive signal. If none does, S17-22's null result is confirmed and extended.

This is `[ACTIVE-MRH]` work, but sequenced after the design-spec is sharpened to avoid the redundancy trap. The deduplication with `explorations/2026-05-15-cellular-automaton-discrete-grid-physics.md` Stage 1 and whitepaper Appendix A Open Math Problem #1 is even more useful now — same target, sharpened spec.

## 4. Stop-conditions / reframe triggers

When autonomous sessions produce any of the following, **stop and reframe** rather than continuing:

- **Verdict-shaped framing of in-flight work**: "X is established / proven / refuted / failed" without that being a closed audit finding. Rewrite as MRH-relationship status update.
- **Definite-article framings**: "the X valley / the bottleneck / the answer to". Linguistic shift signals closure attractor. Rewrite as "this update nudges credence on hypothesis A up, B down, C unchanged."
- **Verdict-shaped meta-narratives on cycle outputs**: "charitable vs skeptical reading wins?" / "does this win the framework's case?" Same closure attractor at framework-evaluation scale. Rewrite as pragmatic inventory.
- **Promotion-to-`✅`-status during stewardship**: nothing can be honestly characterized as "established" at the current stewardship stage. If you find yourself reaching for `✅`, that's the signal to flag.
- **Treating critique-cycle outputs as the unit of progress**: external reviews update credences on multiple parallel hypotheses; none confirms or refutes. Don't collapse the parallel-paths space into a binary judgment.
- **Audit-finding overwrite**: if an autonomous session frames the substrate reformulation as undoing S637/S638/S660A/S661/S663B/S665/S666, that's incorrect. Audit findings stand below the reformulation.
- **Single-data-point story-building**: same closure-attractor surfaced one scale up. Use the **parallel-faith-hypotheses** discipline — every data point nudges credences on multiple competing hypotheses, none confirms or refutes.

Each stop-condition fires reframe → flag → continue with corrected framing. No silent continuation through closure-shaped output.

## 5. What this frame doc does NOT do

- It does not specify what to think — the parallel-paths space is open. It specifies the *shape* of valid output (MRH-relationship taxonomy; Findings vs Framings; audit findings stand; read the parent corpus).
- It does not foreclose new content. Resurfaced material was the recent pattern; genuinely-added content remains possible. The discipline is to mark which is which.
- It does not impose verdicts on the substrate reformulation. The reformulation is `[ACTIVE-MRH]`; its outcome is open; Phase 1 sim is the binary-outcome step. Autonomous sessions should attack the work, not pre-judge it.
- It does not declare the framework wins or loses. Stewardship of a vision along parallel paths is the mode; the eventual shape is not knowable from inside the cycle.
