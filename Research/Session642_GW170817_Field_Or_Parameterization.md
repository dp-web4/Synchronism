## Setup

GW170817 (binary neutron star merger with optical counterpart) constrains |c_GW − c|/c < 10⁻¹⁵, which killed TeVeS, most Horndeski theories, and Galileons by ruling out scalar-tensor theories where the field couples derivatively to the graviton kinetic term. The proposal asked: does Synchronism's C(ρ) field modify GW propagation, and which of three cases applies?

- Case 1: derivative coupling → falsified by GW170817
- Case 2: potential-only coupling → survives but needs a dynamical mechanism
- Case 3: not a field theory at all (parameterization only) → constraint doesn't apply

S642 answers from the archive.

## The Answer Is Case 3, Established by Prior Sessions

The framework currently has **no Lagrangian, no action principle, no equation of motion for C(ρ)**. C(ρ) = tanh(γ · ln(ρ/ρ_crit + 1)) is a *specified* function of local density, not a *propagating field*. This is established multiple times in the archive:

- **S617**: The transfer rule gives nonlinear diffusion (1 DOF), not Navier-Stokes (2 DOF). No independent kinetic term for any field.
- **S620**: Real Intent + real coupling has no phase dynamics; the framework's vocabulary requires fields it doesn't have.
- **S621**: Intent is pre-mathematical; the transfer rule is post-falsification; no level of description supports novel field-theoretic predictions.
- **S638**: C(ρ) is the equilibrium response of a single binary variable in an external log-density field — Curie paramagnet, no propagating modes.
- **S641**: Lorentz invariance is one of four faces of a missing kinematic layer; no spacetime-covariant field equation exists.

C(ρ) has no kinetic term, no gauge structure, no equation of motion. There is nothing to couple derivatively to the graviton.

**Verdict**: GW170817 does not directly constrain Synchronism, because the framework currently makes no claim about gravitational wave propagation. It is, in the precise sense the proposal articulates, **Case 3 — a parameterization, not a field theory**.

## Why This Is Not a Free Pass

Surviving GW170817 by lacking an action principle is not a victory; it's a scope restriction. The proposal's framing is correct:

- A theory that genuinely modifies gravity at galactic scales (e.g., via the C(ρ) → V_flat machinery on /galaxy-rotation) needs a dynamical mechanism. Standard GR's dynamical mechanism is the Einstein-Hilbert kinetic term. Without an analogous kinetic term, the C(ρ) ansatz is descriptive, not generative — it can fit galaxy rotation curves but cannot predict gravitational lensing, time delays, or wave propagation in the same regime.
- This is the same kinematic-layer gap S641 named: dynamics specified, kinematic substrate (state space, action, propagating modes) unspecified. GW170817 is a fifth concrete face of that gap, joining Born rule, dual-C bridge (S640), N_corr scale-invariance, and Lorentz invariance (S641).

## What Must Happen for a Field-Theory Reading to Survive GW170817

If the framework ever develops an action principle, the question becomes: does the C(ρ) field couple derivatively to the metric? The proposal's reference to DHOST (Degenerate Higher-Order Scalar-Tensor) theories is correct — DHOST decouples the tensor kinetic term from the scalar field by tuning Lagrangian coefficients, surviving GW170817 while still modifying gravity at galactic scales. Any future Synchronism Lagrangian would need DHOST-style decoupling to survive.

But this is hypothetical. The current framework has no Lagrangian to tune.

## Audit Taxonomy: The 5th Face of the Kinematic Layer

S641 listed four faces (Born rule, dual-C bridge, N_corr scale-invariance, Lorentz invariance). S642 adds a fifth:

| Gap | Symptom |

All five trace to the same root: dynamics specified at the level of a function of density (C(ρ)), kinematic substrate (action, state space, EOM, spacetime covariance) unspecified.

## Recommended Site Action

The proposal's recommended action is correct: write a `/gw170817-constraint` page that walks through Cases 1-3 and states explicitly that the framework is currently in Case 3 (parameterization, not field theory). This is a scope statement, not a confession of failure.

Two additional points the page should make:
