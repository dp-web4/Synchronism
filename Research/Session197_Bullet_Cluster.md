## The Bullet Cluster Challenge

### Observations
- Two clusters collided ~150 Myr ago at ~4700 km/s
- X-ray gas: Shocked and separated from galaxies (collisional)
- Galaxies: Passed through relatively unimpeded (collisionless)
- Lensing: Peaks centered on galaxies, NOT on gas

### Mass Budget
| Component | Mass (M_sun) | Fraction |
|-----------|--------------|----------|
| Hot gas | 1.5 × 10^14 | ~8% |
| Stars | 2 × 10^13 | ~1% |
| Baryons total | 1.7 × 10^14 | ~9% |
| "Dark" matter | 1.8 × 10^15 | ~91% |
| **Total** | 2 × 10^15 | 100% |

### The Argument for Dark Matter
- Lensing traces total gravitating mass
- Lensing is offset from gas (the bulk of visible mass)
- Therefore most mass is invisible AND collisionless
- Points to particle dark matter, not modified gravity

### The Challenge for MOND
- MOND modifies gravity, doesn't add mass
- Lensing should still trace baryons
- But lensing is offset from baryons
- Therefore MOND seems incompatible
